Goals of game
Goal type could be one of the following and depends on selected scenario: become a leader of a country, keep a crown, defeat country, join army, defeat army, reach the land, own the land, own structure, marry desired person, get title, get rank, be a religion leader, convert all to religion, kill a noble. There should be different scenarios with different goals. A player could be either the ruler of the country or some noble in given scenario. The game should have a database where the initial data of scenarios are stored. The progress of the selected scenario should be saved each turn and rewrite previous records, so it should not be possible to cheat by doing something to check what will happen, then reloading from the previous state before that action and use this information for further actions. The game should support only one selected scenario at a time, launching another scenario should drop the progress of previous scenario.
Countries and lands
There should be a list of countries that can be allies, enemies or neutral to each other and a list of lands of those countries. Not all the lands should be neighbors of each other so there should be info on which land each land borders. The land consists of a central city (with constructions) or village (with constructions) or some kind of terrain (with constructions). The lands could be owned either by monasteries or by nobles or could be owned by the ruler of the country.  The river or the river with ford is one of the types of terrain of the land. Rivers flow through the lands and have some sequence of the lands they flow through. Building a dam leads to the draining of the river below the stream and transforms further lands to the lands with terrain type “stream” that is like terrain type “field”. The collapse of the dam will result in a devastating flood that will kill everyone and destroy everything downstream on the river after the collapsed dam. Rivers should start from swamps or from mountains and should end up in seas. 
Structures
The towns and villages and other lands could contain structures that could be ordered to be built by the owner of the land or by mayor or by guild (that is owned by the owner of the land). Each structure needs some effort and builders to be built. Complexity is an effort for building or destroying the structure and it could be processed partially in several turns. 1 builder can produce 1 point of effort per turn.  Each builder requires tools to build or destroy any structure. The structure could be destroyed either by the builder if free access is set for the structure or by heavy artillery or by an engineer (via undermining using tunnel or via direct mining of the structure) in other cases. Some of them could be destroyed by flood and/or fire. Some structures activate effects when built (there is a mapping table). The structures are divided into groups. The first group is related to housing and contains the house, the farmhouse, and tavern.  Farmhouses could be built only in villages and additionally give the vegetables. The second group is related to gathering resources and contains mines, sawmills, farms and cattle farms. The third group is related to production and contains the mint, bakery, dairy, butchery, jewelry workshop, brewery, distillery, fabric workshop, clothing workshop, forge, ropes workshop, artillery depot, weapon workshop, arsenal, watermill, mill, carriage and wagons workshop, furniture workshop, cooperage, pottery, shipyard, warehouse. The fourth group is related to moving improvements and contains road, bridge, dam. The fifth group is related to religion and contains monasteries and temples and great temples. The sixth group is related to defense and contains palisade, fortification, complex fortification, and stationary fort made of wagons. The seventh group is related to messaging and contains dove house and post office. The eighth group is related to nobility and contains a manor, palace and military school. The ninth group is related to water supply and contains well and fountain.
Professions
Each farmer needs 1 scythe to gather crops and hemp or 1 agricultural tool to gather grapes and milk and wool and produces 5 points of effort per turn. Each builder requires 1 tool and produces 2 efforts per turn. Each lumberjack requires 1 axe and produces 2 efforts per turn. Each miner requires 1 tool and produces 2 efforts per turn. Each craftsman needs 1 tool and provides 2 efforts per turn. Each fishermen requires 1 boat and 1 net and produces 50 points of effort per turn. Each resource requires some effort to produce and requires miners for everything except wood. Wood should be gathered by lumberjacks. 
Resources, weapons and goods
Resources depend on the land, are local and should be gathered by miners for all resources except wood – this needs lumberjack. Miners work on mines and lumberjacks work on sawmill. If wood gathering is too extensive there should be penalty and reforestation need. Mines have limited resources, and if they are depleted, new exploration is necessary to create a new mine. Resources should be stored in local warehouses, if there is not enough place they should be stored w/o warehouse but with some % of loss per 60 turns. Resources could be stolen by bandits if these resources are not guarded. Resources should contain wood (1 piece of wood= 1 volume-weight), ship wood (1 piece of ship wood= 10 volume-weight and could be split to 10 wood), stone (1 piece of stone = 10 volume-weight), gold (1 piece of gold = 500 volume-weight), silver (1 piece of silver = 200 volume-weight), copper (1 piece of copper = 200 volume-weight), iron (1 piece of iron = 200 volume-weight), coal (1 piece of coal = 1 volume-weight, Requires bags or barrels for storage), clay (1 piece of clay = 10 volume-weight), salt (1 piece of salt = 1 volume-weight, requires bags or barrels for storage) and lead (1 piece of lead = 200 volume-weight). Food resources are gathered by farmers and contain crops (require bags or barrels for storage. 1 piece of crop = 1 volume-weight), grapes (1 piece of grapes = 1 volume-weight), cows, horses, sheep and pigs. Straw is collected automatically with crops in equal numbers and could be used as forage. 1 piece of straw = 1 volume weight. Fish is collected by fishermen (specific type of craftsman) on rivers or on sea. Crops and grapes and hemp (1 piece of hemp = 1 volume-weight) are gathered on the farms, gathering of them is limited to spring and autumn. Cows, sheep, pigs and horses require 2 cows, 2 sheep, 2 pigs and 2 horses to start herd with. Each herd has a maximum of 30 cows or horses and up to 50 sheep or pigs and when this limit is reached a new herd is created from leftovers. Cows and horses and sheep give 150% herd growth, and pigs give 300% herd growth each 200 turns. All domestic animals need cattle farms. Transporting animals between the lands requires herds and farmers (1 farmer per herd). Cows provide milk. Sheep provide wool. All domestic animals give meat and skins if killed. Each piece of goods needs some effort to produce and needs some craftsman (there is a mapping of craftsman to goods type). Production of goods depends on available resources. Guilds are the union of craftsmen of specific profession in the town. Weather
There should be weather that varies from land to land and seasons for all lands that should impact the weather. Weather could impact the battles and harvest, and the list of weather types depends on season and terrain type (e.g. snowy weather is possible in mountains also for other seasons than winter). Flood is triggered if rainy weather occurs more than 60 turns in a row and provides 10 to 50 points of damage to structures that are not protected with fortifications and destroys harvest that is on the fields. Drought is triggered if shiny weather occurs more than 60 turns in a row and destroys harvest that is on the fields and with some chance starts fire that damages structures 10 to 50 points of damage. Weather type list includes Shiny, Rainy, Foggy, Windy, Snowy.
Population
The land should have a population of citizens which should be grouped into households that live in houses in towns and farmhouses in villages. The population could be rural or from towns, there should be stratification of income levels (poor, medium, rich). Towns and villages should have mayors that are elected and are responsible for tax collection and for building the constructions in the village or city. Mayor may be not from nobles. There should be nationalities. Population could be conscribed to the army by recruiting agents and after demobilization, part of them (no more than 25%) become mercenaries. Population should have a level of satisfaction and could revolt if the level is too low (impacted by several factors). Rebellon leads to the creation of the rebellious army equipped with weapons from the rebellious land. Population is the origin of all actors like spies, nobles, assassins, recruiting agents, monks, scouts, bards.
Religion
There should be several religions, some of them should be main for nationality and main for country. Religions should have leaders that are elected (may be not from nobles), and those leaders should not participate in war directly but impact the politics via excommunication from the church, asks to the rulers to stop/begin the war, convert everybody in the country to their religion etc. Religious leaders and monastery leaders should not be married if their religion prohibits it. Religions could allow either 1 spouse or many spouses. There should be ranks hierarchy for religions, religion leader should be elected among the higher rank priests. Hierarchies should have same number of ranks for different religions but different descriptions of each rank. Example of hierarchy is priest, abbot, cardinal, pope (higher rank that is nominated to religion leader). Each rank assignment should be approved by religion leader and could be given as a reward for work like conversion of the nobles, conversion of people of the land, active participation in religious courts etc. For monks and for lower-level priests there should be only characteristics like sex, nationality and land of origin due to performance issues. Higher rank priests should have same list of traits as nobles. Preferred way of travelling for high rank priests is in carriages. The religious leader of the main religion of the country should crown the ruler of the country. Each religion should have monks and some of them could travel, and others could live in local monasteries and others could be priests in local temples. Travelling monks could be used as spies to collect information about the land. Each monastery should have some ruler that should not participate in wars and should be a subordinate to the religion leader. Each religion should be tolerant or intolerant to other religions and there could be clashes based on this if those religions are present in the same land and one of them is oppressed. There could be assassination attempts of the priests of the religion by religious fanatics or assassins. Conversion of nobles of the land to other religion is done by monks or priest of different ranks if ordered and is blessed by the religion leader. Conversion of population is done for a long time; it increases chance of mutiny significantly however impacts much better on religion leader satisfaction than conversion of the nobles. Satisfaction of religious needs for people of specific religion is done by priest or higher rank priests in local temple or by monks.
Nobles
There should be nobles of different nationalities in the lands, approximately 5% of population, all the rulers of the countries should be nobles and could be either men or women. Some of the nobles should be favorites of the ruler of the country. Nobles could be either men or women. Nobles are the main force of the armies. Nobles in the army should be paid by the commander of the army once per 60 turns; if the army is the army of the ruler, the ruler should pay them salary once per 60 turns. The ruler of the country should not pay to the nobles that are not part of his army. Nobles should have a level of respect from other nobility and from the population, should have some level of fear of them, should be included in politics and war. Weapons should be bought by them for themselves and for their soldiers if they have them except mercenaries. High level nobles may have their army and should support it if they have it. Nobles should have different levels of thirst of power, strength, dignity, caution, superstition, beauty, culture and religiosity, some of them should not accept bribes, betrayal, should not execute captives, others are ok with it. Preferred way of travelling for high level nobility is in carriages, when in battle – mounted. All nobles may have families that are also included in politics. Women nobles should not be the part of the army, but higher rank women nobles could (not must) participate directly in battles as commanders if they are strong and nobody else could be nominated, otherwise they nominate leadership to their husband or their noble subordinate. Subordinate nobles may (but not must) be rewarded by land or the next title for their service. In this case the land could be given by the master noble, the title could be granted only by the ruler of the country and the master could ask the ruler to give the title to this noble. The higher title nobles could be leaders of armies or units. The lowest level nobles that start their career should be ordinary soldiers and should not have any characteristics except nationality and religion and land of origin due to performance restrictions and should get them when promoted to the next title - this could happen if they are brave in battle and did remarkable impact on result. In such cases the ruler could promote some of them with further titles. For women nobles of the lowest title granting next title is the part of the marriage to noble of higher title. 
Granting nobility to ordinary people and titles system 
Rulers can grant nobility to a person if they require a new noble, but these nobles would not be respected by true nobles. Same for bastards. Bastards have more thirst for power. Granting nobility requires assignment of the lowest title and may be (but not mandatory) rewarded by land or structures. Titles have some sequence that ends with final. Some titles do not have a sequence and are granted as a reward. Noble of title cannot be subordinate to noble of lower or equal title. If title requires it, nobles become subordinate directly to rulers of the country. 
Prestige of the noble
The higher noble’s prestige increases if they are part of the country’s ruler’s court. To be part of the court of the ruler, the noble should spend money on unique carriages, furniture, clothes, tableware, should participate all the balls that are organized by the ruler of the country and should be accepted by the ruler. Spending money requires more tax collection and more usage of resources of the owned lands. The ruler’s prestige increases if his court is bigger. Also, higher nobles’ prestige increases if they are nominated for one of the positions like chancellor, coin holder, defense commander.
Dignity defense system
There should be a system of defending the dignity of the nobles and their families. It could include duels (preferred for men) or trying to kill the offender via help of assassin (preferred by women) or asking the court for help (lowest priority). The duel must happen or the noble that was called for a duel gets ashamed in case the caller of the duel is the same title or higher as the noble that was called. The duel could happen (but not mandatory) in case the caller for the duel has lower title and could result in using assassin to kill the caller if he is too rude. If one of the participants is woman, she should nominate the defender and if nobody found she should reject the duel and hiring of assassin could be done. Duels may be banned by the ruler of the country, but they will still take place secretly with the consequence that if the duelists are caught, they may be suspected of attempted murder with further consequences. An attempted murder could be committed if someone blackmailed a noble. If the ruler of the country is offended by his subordinate, he could order to jail or behead the offender. If the ruler of the country is offended by the noble that belong to other country, he asks other country’s leader to punish that noble and if he does not punish the war could begin. If the offender is the rule of other country, the war should begin.
Relationships and love system
If a noble or a priest loves someone, they may give their beloved gifts, use the services of bards, and invite their beloved to a ball, hunt, or festival. The level of success depends on the noble/priest person's beauty and the amount of attention they give their beloved, as well as whether the person is married. If a person is married and accepts love, this could be a reason for a duel or assassination if discovered. 
Audience with the noble (including ruler of the country)
There should be a possibility of asking the noble for audience where some messages could be shared or some proposals could be conducted, or some requests could be made. The noble could accept or reject the audience request and could react differently to these messages, proposals and requests that are shared with him while the audience. There could be an attempt to murder while the audience with different levels of success depending on the number of people in the room.
Hiring different actors in the game
To hire spies, assassins, tax collectors, recruiting agents, merchants, the player could visit the tavern where he could find all these actors that are in this land and could (not must) pay in advance for their job. The player could give them orders after hiring. Or the player could make an order to chancellor to hire spies or assassins, coin holder to hire tax collectors or merchants, defense commander to hire recruiting agents. The player should give them money for this hiring. There could be an attempt to murder with different levels of success depending on the availability of hired guards if the player is the target victim of the assassin.
Messaging
Messages are the core mechanics of the game and could be done via letters or in oral form. All the information should be taken from the messages and should be interpreted by the actors, no total information should be used and collected in the game.  No decision by any actors in the game should be taken from information that is not part of the messages that were received by them. Messages could contain orders to collect taxes, orders to collect/disband the army, orders to sell/buy some goods or resources, orders to the already existing army units to attack the land or to defend the land, information about lands like number of forces there, level of loyalty, amount of goods and their prices, order to the spy to check loyalty of the noble (could be done via observing or via interception of his/her correspondence) or to organize rebellion in the land, offers of war and peace with details like marriage proposals if accepted, calls for a duel, proposals to betray the sovereign for some reward, proposals to join the alliance of nobles to get the throne, proposals to get lands of the noble if supports the alliance of nobles to get the throne, orders to join the council, orders with nomination of recipient to chancellor or coin holder or defense commander, proposals to join a ball or a festival or a hunt, orders to print the coins. Letters could be protected by stamps that could be broken and restamped with some level of accuracy by the spies that can read the letter and if needed change the content. Letters could be sent via messengers and doves and should be kept or destroyed. If the letter is kept for further usage, it could be stolen (with some probability depending on the guard level) by the opponent using a spy. Letters could be intercepted and could be used in politics like Casus Belli, decrease of loyalty etc. Level of chance of interception is higher if the message is sent through the hostile lands or there is a spy with the order to intercept the correspondence, or messenger is bribed or killed. Oral messages require messenger and could be revealed to enemies of the noble if the messenger is not loyal or the message is against the family of the messenger or the messenger himself. Both letters and oral messages could be fake. Messages could be done by monks, priests including religion leaders, spies, assassins, army commanders, unit commanders, nobles including rulers of the countries, merchants, mayors, ambassadors, scouts, recruiting agents, bards, all of them could be used as messengers. There could be also messengers from post offices, these could be bribed to reveal the letter of the message. The post office provides up to 20 messengers and horses for them. When the horse is tired it could be changed for the fresh one from the post office by paying money. Also, the post office should give horses to royal messengers by request. The messenger has a target location, which is told to him by the sender where the recipient should be, and if the messenger arrived there and did not find the recipient, he could, depending on the senders' orders, either wait or move on to the next known location, which is told by people from the current land if the recipient did not leave at night, until he finds the recipient. Doves are sent to a specific location and if the recipient is not found there, they do not continue searching for the recipient. Any message should include "Deliver personally", "Via messenger", "Via dove" and should default to the "Via messenger" even if it is the same land for the sender and recipient. The type of the message should be "Sealed letter" by default. "Deliver personally" should be available only if the sender and recipient is in the same land. All deliveries inside 1 land should take 1 turn except the contact personally but it has to be limited to up to 10 visits per turn and if this in-person delivery of the message is to the person the title of which is lower than his own this visit lowers his (visitor) prestige by creating a corresponding gossip except the visit to the chancellor, coin holder or defense commander but if the title of the noble is equal or higher it must not impact. Personal visits are triggered for the player when the specific button on the screen is clicked. All recipient target land should be taken from the knowledge of the person that sends the order or invitation. If the recipient location is unknown the sending of the message should be not possible.
Politics and succession rules 
Politics should be marriage-based, succession should be based on the rights of elders and the possibility to disagree and start war or rebellion if so. The ruler of the country should be ordained by the leader of the religion that is official in the country and should have a symbol of his power (crown etc.). The ruler of the country could use either his or his subordinate noble family for politics and this could be either the factor of increase or decrease of loyalty of the given noble or his family that could disagree due to different view on the marriage of his family members or not enough beauty of the candidate and if loyalty is too low start revolt or sabotage in some way. There could be possibilities of hiring the assassin to poison or kill the opponent in other way (success depends on level of guarding and level of cautious of the target). If the assassin was not paid, the hirer becomes the target of this assassin. If assassin fails, there is a chance of his arrest or death. The official diplomacy should be done based on ambassadors that should reach the land of the corresponding noble for whom the official proposal is done. The answer could be sent by dove from ambassador or by himself in person or left w/o any answer if ambassador is imprisoned/killed. The ambassador could be appointed among the messengers. The unofficial politics could be done via messages sent by messengers or doves. 
Move rules
1 turn switches night to day or vice versa. Usually everybody rests and does not move at night but there could be exceptions (such as moving at night to avoid combat during a retreat, etc.). Nighttime gives + to stealth. All movements is done via list of standard routes that have the list of lands they cover (like route 1 has start point at land A, ends at land Z requires to go through the land B, land C, land D) and are used by actors while moving in the next way: move to land A using route 1, then move to land B via route 2 etc. until the target land is reached. There could be also secret shorter, more optimized routes for part of the lands that could be revealed to the actors by locals if they are loyal and got paid for that. The secret routes could also be a trap and end up in swamps with a 10% probability of losing part of the army and supplies or in the ambush of the local bandits if the population is hostile. Resources and money and goods except artillery could be transferred between lands via convoys of wagons that could be guarded by armies of different sizes. Artillery could be transferred using primarily a horse or a cow in exception cases. 1 wagon could transfer up to 1000 volume-weight and can hold up to 2 light artilleries. 1 wagon needs 2 horses or cows and 1 person to ride. 1 horse/cow can transport up to 200 volume-weight on it. 1 boat could transfer up to 1000 volume-weight. Speed on foot is 1 land to land per turn. Artillery speed = speed on foot. Speed with horse = 2 lands in galloping mode and 1 land in ordinary mode per turn. A horse in galloping mode can’t ride more than 1 turn. Speed of boat = speed on foot. Speed of wagon or carriage or expensive carriage = speed on foot. Moving 2 turns in a row leads to tired state and requires 1 turn to rest to return to normal state, moving 3 turns in a row leads to exhaustion and needs 1 turn to rest to return to normal state. Moving 6 days in a row w/o rest force to rest anyway. Lack of food or water leads to exhaustion that cannot be solved via rest but only via eating food and drinking water. When the army or convoy of wagons or herd or other actors (spies, nobles, assassins, recruiting agents, monks, scouts, bards) move they should check if there is a not drained river in the land where they are moving to. If there is a river all actors like spies, nobles, assassins, recruiting agents, monks, scouts, bards are grouped in the units to cross the river. After that the partial move is performed. If there is a river ford/crossing the move of up to 20 units/herds/convoys/carriages per turn is performed.  If there is a bridge/dam the move of up to 40 units/herds/convoys/carriages per turn is performed. If there are only boats available, the move of up to 1 unit/herd per 1 boat available is performed. If there is a river and no boats there, the move could be done (but it’s up to the actor that moves) with 80% chance to drown while crossing. In case the army cannot cross the river in 1 turn, the army divides into 2 until all army moves to the target location. If an army is crossing a river, then the top priority should be given to the crossing of the higher nobility and their property, followed by army units or army convoys; other actors move only after the army has crossed the river. If the capacity of the crossing/bridge/dam/ford/boats available is not enough for all moving actors, maximum actors move and the rest of actors that have not moved yet wait for another turn to try to cross the river.
Consuming supplies mechanics
1 citizen, 1 actor (noble, monk, spy, assassin etc.), 1 soldier, 1 horse and other domestic animals needs 1 food satiety and 1 water per 2 turns, they could die with 10 % chance after 10 turns if not have food or water. Turns to die from famine = 72 turns, from thirst = 14 turns. Hunger and thirst turn the condition into exhaustion, which cannot be relieved by rest, but only by eating and drinking. Spies, monks, assassins, scouts, merchants, recruiting agents, bards, mayors can take food automatically if they are in the village/town with enough food and water. Soldiers can get food from citizens or convoys that do not belong to their army or from herds, but this will decrease the satisfaction level of the origin/target lands of these convoys/herds and if there is famine or thirst it could lead to revolt of the origin/target lands of these convoys/herds when news reaches them. Food need is satisfied for horses and other animals automatically if the end of move is on pastureland and it is not winter. Water need is automatically satisfied for animals if the end of the turn is on the water containing terrain. The mood of the people decreases if they eat animal food. Usage of alcohol lowers caution levels to 50% and increases mood if soldiers do not eat animal food. The army can take food that could be transported by soldiers, and water without convoys for 4 turns. Each actor (noble, monk, spy, assassin etc.) has a flask that does not need to be bought and gives water per 4 turns. Water in flasks and in barrels could be refilled if there is a well or fountain or river in the location where the army stays. If water is poisoned there is a 50% chance of death for both humans and animals each turn, they drink poisoned water. The crops should be cooked before being eaten by humans, and this requires cauldrons, fireplaces and some other resources like salt, water, fish and meat. The meat and fish could be dried (which requires 14 turns) or should be cooked. Some of the food satisfies both food and water needs. There is some food that is served to animals. Food that does not need the cauldron for cooking could be transported by soldiers w/o convoys. Food that is cooked in cauldrons should be cooked and eaten immediately; others could be stored. The food receipts are the next ones. 1 piece of porridge requires 1 piece of crops and 1 piece of water and 1 piece of salt and needs a cauldron with fireplace for cooking and gives 5 points of food satiety. Porridge with meat or fish requires 1 piece of crops and 1 piece of meat or fish and 1 piece of water and 1 piece of salt and needs a cauldron with fireplace for cooking and gives 10 points of food satiety. 1 piece of soup needs 1 piece of water and 1 piece of meat or fish and 1 piece of salt and needs a cauldron with fireplace for cooking and gives 4 points of food satiety and 1 point of water satiety. If these food recipes are cooked without salt, they are considered not tasty (lowers mood) but keep the same level of food and water satieties. 1 piece of grapes gives 1 food satiety. 1 piece of bread gives 4 food satiety. 1 piece of cheese gives 3 food satiety. 1 piece of milk or water or beer or wine gives 1 water satiety. 1 dried meat or 1 dried fish gives 2 points of food satiety. 1 piece of straw is animal food and gives 1 point of food satiety. 1 piece of uncooked crops could be eaten by animals and gives 1 food satiety.
Economics and Trade
Money could be made via printing coins and is done in the capital by mint if the order to print coins is received. Coins are made from silver or gold, a piece of silver or gold results in 10000 silver or gold coins correspondingly. Making money in different places is considered a crime. Coins should be collected via taxes from owned lands; they are collected periodically. Taxes could be also gathered from trade – each deal gives some tax. In some cases, coins could be offered by actors like spies, nobles, assassins, recruiting agents, monks, scouts, bards as a present or could be part of offerings in negotiations. The present increases the loyalty of the ruler to the actor that did the present. If taxes are too high for the population, it could plan a revolt. If taxes on trade are too extensive the black market grows. Money is local and should be transferred via convoys of wagons between the lands in the place where it should be used. There is no global money for the actors in the game. Trade is performed via merchants that sell their goods and buy needed goods or resources in the lands or overseas territories. Overseas trade gives all kinds of resources but needs ships to perform such trade. 1 ship carries up to 20000 volume-weight. Lands produce typically specific resources and goods for sale and require other resources/goods for themselves. All trade is done on the several local markets, and on the periodical markets (these are huge). There are lists of standard trade routes that cover the list of markets involved and what goods in what numbers are to be sold and to be bought on each. Merchants are assigned to these standard routes. The player can hire the merchant to sell/buy the goods/resources for him. Merchants use wagons to transport their goods and money. The local market of the land cannot provide unlimited amounts of desired product and cannot hold more than some limit. The market of the land does not sell the product if the amount of the product is low and is critical for the survival of the land. Nobles can gather periodically taxes also for themselves from owned lands. Taxes could be gathered in resources, goods or money. Trade route stops if there is a war in one of the lands the market of this route is located, or in case there is a war between countries, and the route includes markets of these countries. 
Battles and battlefields
Army requires resources and goods that are transported with the army via convoys of wagons or could be transported in little amounts by the army for short term campaigns. Army contains several units that are led by nobles or other actors. Units contain soldiers of different types, and each soldier is an ordinary man or noble of specific nationality and religion and land of origin. Army of non-nobles could be collected for garrison service or as auxiliary units or could arrive due to rebellion. Garrison service armies fulfil also police functions. Units of non-nobles typically are less effective and have less morale in battle than units of nobles. Units of nobles may be mixed with non-nobles who are their servants. The unit strength is a maximum of 40 soldiers. If there is artillery in the army it should be a part of some unit/units. If there are some convoys in the army, they should be a part of some unit/units. Units could be gathered via conscription of the ordinary men population or nobles via recruiting agents (in this case leader of the army should give them corresponding weapons and goods) or via hiring mercenaries via recruiting agents (limited amounts in the land, appear from experienced soldiers after demobilization). Types of soldiers are pikeman without armor (needs 1 pike), light pikeman (needs 1 pike and 1 light armor), pikeman (needs 1 pike and 1 armor), pikeman with saber without armor (needs 1 pike and 1 saber), light pikeman with saber (needs 1 pike, 1 saber and1 light armor), pikeman with saber (needs 1 pike, 1 saber and 1 armor), musketeer (needs 1 gun and 1 saber), musketeer without saber (needs 1 gun), armored musketeer without saber (needs 1 gun and 1 light armor), armored musketeer (requires 1 gun, 1 saber and 1 light armor), swordsman (requires 1 saber), swordsman with shield (requires 1 saber and 1 shield), armored swordsman (requires 1 saber and 1 light armor), heavy armored swordsman (requires 1 saber and 1 armor), armored swordsman with shield (needs 1 saber, 1 light armor and 1 shield), heavy armored swordsman with shield (needs 1 saber, 1 armor and 1 shield), archer (required 1 bow), archer with saber (required 1 bow and 1 saber), archer with shield (needs 1 bow and 1 shield and 1 saber), armored archer (needs 1 bow and 1 light armor), armored archer with saber (needs 1 bow, 1 saber and 1 light armor), militia (needs 1 war scythe), light armored militia (needs 1 war scythe and 1 light armor), armored militia (needs 1 war scythe and 1 armor), peasants (needs 1 scythe or 1 tool or 1 agricultural tool or 1 axe), light armored peasants (needs 1 light armor and needs 1 scythe or 1 tool or 1 agricultural tool or 1 axe), armored peasants (needs 1 armor and needs 1 scythe or 1 tool or 1 agricultural tool or 1 axe), engineers (requires 1 tool). All of these soldiers could be mounted. All these soldier types could be named differently in different countries. All unarmored soldiers are vulnerable to guns and bows volleys and are weaker than armored soldier types in close combat. Light armor and shields give more advantages in close combat and some levels of protection from bows volley; armor gives some level of protection from guns and bows volleys and gives more advantage in close combat. Pikes and war scythes are more effective in close combat against cavalry and in close combat without entering the enemy ranks. Cavalry attacks can break ranks of enemy infantry without pikes or battle scythes and have a significant impact on morale. Cavalry attacks of soldiers with pikes could break ranks of enemy infantry more effectively. Guns and bows volley could kill horses. Light artillery volley impacts morale and could be used for firing light shells into units. Heavy artillery volley impacts morale significantly and could be used for firing heavy shells into units or bombarding structures. Chance of revolt grows if the message about revolt in the land appears in the army unit with soldiers from that land. If the army unit contains citizens of the revolted land, there is high possibility of revolting in the army unit if it is sent to fight against the rebellion. Mercenaries could change sides or retreat if bribed. Some percent of mercenaries become bandits (consider as an army) after the end of contract if no war occurs and they are not paid enough. Bandits attack convoys if strong enough and hide in the land avoiding battles if forces are stronger than bandit army. Bandits could be exterminated via finding them via scouting and winning the battle against them. Battles are performed on the lands between armies that are on the same land and could last several turns. Battles could be avoided if the army is in stealthy mode and is not big enough. Battles could be conducted either by the player or by nobles or by another actor that is delegated there as a general. Armies in tired state fight 20% less effective than in normal state, armies in exhausted state fight 50 % less effective than in normal state. If one unit runs away there is a high chance of breaking the ranks and retreat of nearby units. The battle must be conducted by each army with a selected sequence of tactics (up to 10 per 1 turn), available in the list studied by the commander, and in battle the sequence of tactics or the tactics themselves can be changed as needed and possible. The change of the tactics could be done, if possible, via messenger to the commander of the unit or via flag for specific unit (if it was agreed before the battle) or via horn for everybody. Horns and flag signals cannot be heard/seen for units that are in close combat. List of tactics depends on terrain. Tactics could use obstacles. There are also commands like charge, retreat that is done via horn to everybody or to specific units (if it was agreed before the battle). Generals could be taught some tactics in military schools or copied from the enemy if observed it. Ambush is one of the tactics and success rates depending on terrain, size of the army, success of the enemy scouts and weather. There should be flags for each unit, losing the flag leads to demoralization. Some flags belong to such famous units, that they make fear in opponents if they observe one. The flags may have their own name, e.g. the royal St. Paul’s flag etc. Army should have morale based on commander experience/respect, amount of food, last battle experience (won/lost/retreat), number of losses, balance of army power with enemy. Army has demoralization if the commander is killed or ran away and has morale boost if commander joins the ranks.  If morale is too low desertion could happen. Participation in the battle increases popularity of the commander if there is a victory, execution of captives increases fear of commander and leads to different reactions from religion leaders. Defeat leads to fleeing of part of the defeated army that becomes bandits. Winner can take a hostage from captured nobles and set a ransom for freeing or keep them imprisoned or execute them or set them free. This impacts fear and popularity. One of the possible battle options is in case there are some fortifications with garrison. Fortification could be (but not guaranteed) stormed successfully with superiority 3 to 1 or higher if same or lower level of experience of attacked unit, 2 to 1 or higher if attacking unit is more experienced. Alternatively, the level of success is lower. Trenches could be built by soldiers using tools for the storming preparation to lower the level of losses; each trench allows safe passing of 3 units before the storm begins and allows building a bastion for artillery closer to the fortifications so the accuracy of volleys increases. If the commander is too arrogant, he can order to storm the fortifications without trenches and if there is a volley from the walls the units could get casualties even before they reach the distance of fire. The attack is done only if there is a possibility of winning and in other cases siege is performed. In case of the siege there could be supplies cut. Sorties are performed by defenders from time to time to break the siege. Fortifications are destroyed via heavy artillery shelling or via digging tunnels and exploding of gunpowder under fortifications by engineers. Tunnels could be used to transfer soldiers inside the enemy fortification or vice versa and could be destroyed via tunneling and exploding the tunnel using gunpowder from opposite direction. Fortifications may contain several levels that should be captured sequentially. The farms and cattle farms could be destroyed as one of the options of war. Some percentage of destruction of the farms is performed anyway if an army of enough size marches through the land that does not have a road. One of the possible options for war is to poison the wells and fountains and rivers on the march of the enemy army. It could be done inside the fortifications if the well or fountain is not protected enough. Poisoning of the water supplies impacts people respect of the commander that ordered it. Battlefield could be a source of loot. If it is not cleared and the dead people’s number is more than 200 it could lead with a 20% chance to poisoned wells and fountains in this land and if dead people are more than 20 will lead to high activity of wild animals that attack herds and convoys in this land (result of attack is 10% chance of dead soldiers and 50% chance of dead domestic animal). Hunting destroys the activity of wild animals. Poisoned water supplies like wells and fountains and rivers last for 400 turns if they are not cleared. It requires 20 turns to clean the water supplies. Treatment of the corpses can lead to different reactions from the religion leaders if fallen are the same religion and country as the leader. Clearing of the battlefield requires time and resources (e.g. for burning wood etc.) 
Positions in the game
The player could nominate a defense commander among his subordinates that is responsible for the hiring of the army, maintenance of fortifications and hiring garrisons. The defense commander could plan the revolt if not loyal enough and could steal some percent of the budget if he is not honest. The defense commander could get an order to hire recruiting agents, to prepare an invasion to specific country or defense from invasion from specific country, hire or disband an army, increase defense of specific land. He/she could request money to fulfil the order.
The player could nominate a coin holder among his subordinates to manage finances. There could be corruption if this noble is not loyal enough or is not honest. The coin holder could get orders like hire tax collectors or merchants, increase or decrease taxes (maybe for all lands or for specific lists of lands), buy or sell some supplies and prepare money to specific turn for the player to conduct some activities. He/she could request money to fulfil the order.
The player could nominate a chancellor among his subordinates to gather all the information and provide reports to rulers; there could be political games if chancellors are not loyal enough. The chancellor should hire spies that gather info for him/her, then sort the news by significance and make the report to the hirer. Some of the news could be hidden or changed by purpose. The chancellor could get order to collect info about specific land, to organize the rebellion in specific land, to kill the actor like spy, merchant etc. and he/she should use available spies or assassins to do this work. Also, the chancellor could get orders to organize a ball or hunting in specific land. He/she could request money to fulfil the order.
Rebellion
In case if there is critically low level of loyalty to the sovereign, there is a famine or critically low level of satisfaction or the nationality or its main religion is oppressed or there is an unfair decision of the court for person that is popular among the population or the person that was guilty and that is hated by people is set free the rebellion could start with 10 % chance per turn. It starts in some land and could spread to others if not defeated. The rebellion creates an army of people of nationalities that are unsatisfied, and these people try to capture arsenals using tools and agricultural tools and scythes and axes and if this is successful, use weapons and armor from the arsenal of the land the rebellion occurs. In some cases, rebellion could be inspired from outside and promoted by some agents like spies that could give initial weapons to the rebellion to capture arsenal. Rebellion has some goal – it could be one of the next: kill the specific noble (including the ruler or judge), kill all nobles of the land, kill specific priest or monk, kill all priests and monks of the land, destroy the country rebellion belongs to, throw out the enemy army that occupies the land, stop oppression of religion, stop oppression of nationality. The goals of an uprising are determined at the beginning of the rebellion and may change over time to more significant if there is no success (from kill one to kill all, from kill all to destroy the country etc.). The rebellion could be defeated by defeating all armies of rebels. After that some rebels from the lands that were part of rebellion become bandits. The rebellion is victorious and ends when all the goals of the rebellion are achieved. The request to forgive rebels is sent to the ruler of the country in such a case and some rebels become bandits. The decision of the country's ruler to forgive the rebels of the rebellion and punish those who caused the rebellion could be an option to end rebellion.
Army at rest
To set up a camp a tent for 4 soldiers and 1 fireplace per 20 soldiers is required. If there is no tent or fireplace for some of the soldiers it lowers the morale of the unit these soldiers belong to. If the soldiers are resting the patrols for guarding could be used. Patrols are more effective if they have a fireplace (each requires 1 piece of wood per turn). The chances of spotting the enemy are 80% if fireplace is there and 30% if it is not. Fireplaces will lead to spotting the army by enemy that is in the same land. If a patrol is there and spotted an enemy, it raises alert, and all army gets ready for battle. The number of patrols per army is 1 per 3 units. If lower, the chance of spotting the enemy lowers to 10% if w/o fireplace and 50% if with.
Entertainments
There should be some entertainment like balls, religious/local festivals, hunting. Participation/organization impacts political decisions and improves popularity and morale and prestige. Inviting bards is required to organize the balls and festivals and may improve local or religious festivals, seniority of bards impact the level of attractiveness of the entertainment. Also, alcohol is required to organize the balls and festivals. The balls could be organized by nobles or by mayors, local festivals occur from time to time, religious festivals depend on religion and have some schedule. Religious festivals should contain some monks and priests from nearby lands and visiting of this festival by religious leader increases attractiveness of it dramatically. 
In case the ball was organized there could be (but not must) scandals among participants that result in the desire to get revenge. In case the person invited another person to the ball and the person agreed the level of relationships increases. The balls are also the source of gossip. The balls should be supported with alcohol like wine and an assassin can poison his victim's alcohol with some level of success if he/she is on the list of attendees.
In case the festival was organized it gives an increase in mood and prestige of all participants. There could be (but not must) scandals between participants that result in the desire for revenge. Also gossip could be collected. In case the person invited another person to the festival and the person agreed the level of relationships increases. Festivals should be fueled by alcohol, such as beer and wine, and an assassin can poison his victim's alcohol with some level of success if he/she is on the list of attendees.
In case the hunting was organized the risk of injuring is applied to each participant. There could be (but not must) also scandals among participants and the desire to get revenge. There could be (but not must) also envy to the most successful hunter and this gives desire to get revenge. If the most successful hunter has invited somebody, the level of relationship increases. Also gossip could be collected. Assassin could try to direct the dangerous animals to his target victim, increasing the chance of injury of the victim if he/she is on the list of participants.
Justice
There should be courts of the country’s ruler, courts of nobles and religious courts. Decisions would be based on loyalty to the judged person, bribery, and the level of religion superstition of the judge in the case of a religious court. Unfair judgement could lead to unrest. There could be a possibility of mutiny for some nationalities in the land if they are oppressed and think the result of the judgement was unfair. There should be loyalty to people of the same nationality in all cases (courts and their decisions, loyalty to the sovereign etc.). Some crimes could stay untouched if the suspect is of the high title and the ruler of the country does not want them to be the suspects. The religious courts are related to witchcraft and suspects should be from the known witches or witchers that are named witch or witcher based on gossips. The decision of the judge in such cases depends on his level of superstition. There should be also public punishments like executions via beheading, burning down, hanging, imprisoning, shooting based on court decisions. Some types of punishment are known as clean; others are not. This could be interpreted as entertainment for some people and should impact morale/fear level depending on who is punished and for what. Some punishments could be based on religion superstition, others not. There could be also punishment via forcingly becoming a monk or nun. Crime types could be murder, attempt to murder, participation in duel, robbery, banditry, revolt, witchcraft, faked message, espionage, counterfeit coins, black market.
User interface
The user interface should contain several views that change based on the actions of the player
First view if the player is in town or village
The first view relates to the location in a town or village. There should be a room with a table in it and all available in this land nominated nobles like chancellor, coin holder and defense commander should sit there. There should be a map on the table that should be used in the second view. Clicking on the available nobles give screen with their report of execution of given orders and ability to give next orders. Clicking on the map opens second view. There should be buttons “End a turn” and “skip several turns” and “return to main menu”. Clicking the button “End a turn” ends a turn, clicking on “skip several turns” gives possibility to put some number of turns to pass before returning to the view. This should stop if there are important messages like declaration of war or peace, request for a duel, battle in current location of the player. Clicking the button “back to main menu” launches twelfth view. There could be also messengers with messages to the player, they could ask the audience and if the player agrees the ninth view call should be done with all messengers. There could be also private audience request that should happen in the room or in the tavern depending on the requestor will. There could be also buttons “join ball”, “join festival”, “join religious festival”, “join hunt” if corresponding entertainments are organized in current location. There could be a button “Go for a duel” if the player is called for a duel and this is the time for it.
First view if the player is not in town or village
There should be a tent instead of the room, everything else should be the same as in first view if the player is in town or village.
Second view 
The second view is opened when there is a click on the map on the first view. It shows the map of the world with different details of terrain depending on the quality of the map with schematic signs of villages, towns, forests, swamps, fields, hills, mountains, roads, rivers and different types of crossings. There should be objects standing on the map that represent known armies with the latest known locations on the map. Clicking on specific land gives a last report on it and allows the player to give orders to move a player there, to move specific army there, to improve defense there, to organize the rebellion there, to collect info about this land, to organize the ball or hunting there that is given to corresponding noble with these responsibilities. There should be back button to first view.

The third view is related to the battle. The player sits in the room with a table on it with the local map and has messengers and doves in front of him. Messengers and doves could be sent to the army units. Also, there are soldiers with horns and with flags which could be ordered to execute general command like retreat. Clicking on the map moves to fourth view. There should be button “End a turn” and “Skip several turns” that should be available after the battle had finished or 10 tactics were conducted. These buttons should have the same logic as in first view. Also, button “return to main menu” should be available with the same logic as in first view.

This view is opened if the player clicked the button “Take a meal” on the first view. The setting is a table at which the player sits, surrounded by a retinue that serves him food. The setting could be either in the room if the land is the town or a village or the tent if it is not. The buttons “Start a meal” and “End a meal” are available. Clicking on “Start a meal” gives options to choose food and drinks from the available meals. Also gossips are conducted. There could be (but not must) scandals that lead to desire of revenge between participants. Poisoning of some meals from menu could be done with low level of success by assassin if the player is on the assassin’s victims list. Also, there may be an attempted murder by some of the people who are at the table with different level of success. Clicking on button “End a meal” returns player to the first view.
