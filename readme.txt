Goals of game
Goal type could be one of the following and depends on selected scenario: become a leader of a country, keep a crown, defeat country, join army, defeat army, reach the land, own the land, own structure, marry desired person, get title, get rank, be a religion leader, convert all to religion, kill a noble. There should be different scenarios with different goals. A player could be either the ruler of the country or some noble in given scenario. The game should have a database where the initial data of scenarios are stored. The progress of the selected scenario should be saved each turn and rewrite previous records, so it should not be possible to cheat by doing something to check what will happen, then reloading from the previous state before that action and use this information for further actions. The game should support only one selected scenario at a time, launching another scenario should drop the progress of previous scenario.
Countries and lands
There should be a list of countries that can be allies, enemies or neutral to each other and a list of lands of those countries. Not all the lands should be neighbors of each other so there should be info on which land each land borders. The land consists of a central city (with constructions) or village (with constructions) or some kind of terrain (with constructions). The lands could be owned either by monasteries or by nobles or could be owned by the ruler of the country.  The river or the river with ford is one of the types of terrain of the land. Rivers flow through the lands and have some sequence of the lands they flow through. Building a dam leads to the draining of the river below the stream and transforms further lands to the lands with terrain type “stream” that is like terrain type “field”. The collapse of the dam will result in a devastating flood that will kill everyone and destroy everything downstream on the river after the collapsed dam. Rivers should start from swamps or from mountains and should end up in seas. 
Structures
The towns and villages and other lands could contain structures that could be ordered to be built by the owner of the land or by mayor or by guild (that is owned by the owner of the land). Each structure needs some effort and builders to be built. Complexity is an effort for building or destroying the structure and it could be processed partially in several turns. 1 builder can produce 1 point of effort per turn.  Each builder requires tools to build or destroy any structure. The structure could be destroyed either by the builder if free access is set for the structure or by heavy artillery or by an engineer (via undermining using tunnel or via direct mining of the structure) in other cases. Some of them could be destroyed by flood and/or fire. Some structures activate effects when built (there is a mapping table). The structures are divided into groups. The first group is related to housing and contains the house, the farmhouse, and tavern.  Farmhouses could be built only in villages and additionally give the vegetables. The second group is related to gathering resources and contains mines, sawmills, farms and cattle farms. The third group is related to production and contains the mint, bakery, dairy, butchery, jewelry workshop, brewery, distillery, fabric workshop, clothing workshop, forge, ropes workshop, artillery depot, weapon workshop, arsenal, watermill, mill, carriage and wagons workshop, furniture workshop, cooperage, pottery, shipyard, warehouse. The fourth group is related to moving improvements and contains road, bridge, dam. The fifth group is related to religion and contains monasteries and temples and great temples. The sixth group is related to defense and contains palisade, fortification, complex fortification, and stationary fort made of wagons. The seventh group is related to messaging and contains dove house and post office. The eighth group is related to nobility and contains a manor, palace and military school. The ninth group is related to water supply and contains well and fountain.
Professions
Each farmer needs 1 scythe to gather crops and hemp or 1 agricultural tool to gather grapes and milk and wool and produces 5 points of effort per turn. Each builder requires 1 tool and produces 2 efforts per turn. Each lumberjack requires 1 axe and produces 2 efforts per turn. Each miner requires 1 tool and produces 2 efforts per turn. Each craftsman needs 1 tool and provides 2 efforts per turn. Each fishermen requires 1 boat and 1 net and produces 50 points of effort per turn. Each resource requires some effort to produce and requires miners for everything except wood. Wood should be gathered by lumberjacks.

Weather
There should be weather that varies from land to land and seasons for all lands that should impact the weather. Weather could impact the battles and harvest, and the list of weather types depends on season and terrain type (e.g. snowy weather is possible in mountains also for other seasons than winter). Flood is triggered if rainy weather occurs more than 60 turns in a row and provides 10 to 50 points of damage to structures that are not protected with fortifications and destroys harvest that is on the fields. Drought is triggered if shiny weather occurs more than 60 turns in a row and destroys harvest that is on the fields and with some chance starts fire that damages structures 10 to 50 points of damage. Weather type list includes Shiny, Rainy, Foggy, Windy, Snowy.
Population
The land should have a population of citizens which should be grouped into households that live in houses in towns and farmhouses in villages. The population could be rural or from towns, there should be stratification of income levels (poor, medium, rich). Towns and villages should have mayors that are elected and are responsible for tax collection and for building the constructions in the village or city. Mayor may be not from nobles. There should be nationalities. Population could be conscribed to the army by recruiting agents and after demobilization, part of them (no more than 25%) become mercenaries. Population should have a level of satisfaction and could revolt if the level is too low (impacted by several factors). Rebellon leads to the creation of the rebellious army equipped with weapons from the rebellious land. Population is the origin of all actors like spies, nobles, assassins, recruiting agents, monks, scouts, bards.
Disease system
The disease system models the spread and impact of illness across lands, armies and populations. Disease spreads through specific vectors and not randomly. No actor has knowledge of germ theory and all understanding is observational and superstition-based. Disease kills more soldiers than combat in prolonged campaigns and creates genuine strategic decisions rather than just passive penalties. All disease and institution names can be changed in scenario configuration to suit the setting.

Disease types
There are several types of disease in the game. The first is a mild waterborne illness caused by armies camping near contaminated water, battlefield dead near water sources where large numbers of dead give a chance of contaminating wells each turn, overcrowded camps with poor sanitation, flood water mixing with waste, and deliberate poisoning by enemies. It spreads slowly across lands over many turns. Mortality is low and recovery without treatment is possible though the disease persists for many turns untreated. All actors are affected but soldiers suffer worst. The army fights less effectively, moves more slowly, loses morale each turn, and consumes more supplies than normal. The population becomes less satisfied, produces less, and pays less tax while the disease is active.
The second waterborne type is more severe and is caused by severely contaminated water, the mild form developing when untreated, mass poisoning events, and flood aftermath. It spreads faster than the mild form. Mortality is moderate and recovery without treatment is difficult. The army loses a significant portion of its combat effectiveness and movement capability, morale drops faster, and commanders must rest armies when the illness becomes serious enough. The population suffers heavier satisfaction and productivity losses and faces a real risk of revolt if the disease goes untreated for long enough.
The third type is contact disease spread through physical contact, shared clothing and bedding, and lice in crowded conditions. It is devastating in winter quarters and siege conditions and is historically the primary killer of armies in long campaigns. Causes include overcrowded winter quarters, shared bedding without washing, siege conditions affecting both sides of a siege, prison camps for captives, and refugee populations in towns. It spreads fast in crowded conditions and doubles in severity roughly every five turns if untreated. Mortality is medium to high. The disease is silent during its incubation period meaning it spreads before symptoms appear. In winter it spreads and kills at twice the normal rate and overcrowded conditions make it significantly worse. The army loses half its combat effectiveness and much of its movement speed and morale when the disease is serious. It spreads to civilian populations in every land the army passes through and commanders must make a decision when the disease reaches a serious level. The population faces severe satisfaction and productivity losses and a high revolt risk and the disease spreads to neighboring lands through trade and movement.
The fourth type is a highly contagious spotted skin disease with high mortality in populations that have not been exposed before. Contact with infected persons causes it, as do foreign travelers from regions without previous exposure and ships arriving from distant regions. It spreads very fast along routes between lands. Mortality is high and populations with no historical exposure die at higher rates than those with partial exposure. Survivors gain permanent immunity. It can devastate army regiments with no previous exposure while veterans of earlier outbreaks are immune. In populations it causes massive satisfaction drops, permanently removes skilled workers through death, and disrupts trade as towns quarantine themselves.
The fifth type is the great epidemic, the most feared disease of the era. It arrives via ships and trade caravans and spreads from ports inland along existing trade routes. It can end campaigns, destroy cities, and topple governments. It spreads at medium speed along trade routes but fast within dense populations. Mortality is catastrophic ranging from roughly a third to more than half of infected, influenced by population density, season where summer is the peak period and winter slows it, quality of medical care which provides a small improvement, and effectiveness of quarantine which makes a significant difference. The disease can recur in waves and survivors have only partial protection against future outbreaks. The disease comes in three forms: the standard form spread by rats and fleas which is slower and slightly less lethal, an airborne form which spreads faster and kills more and which is favored by winter conditions, and a blood infection form which is the fastest killing and the rarest. Armies cannot campaign safely through affected lands and face a chance each turn of the disease entering the army if stationed there. Once present in an army it spreads and kills like the contact disease but at plague mortality rates. Commanders refuse to enter plague lands due to the effect on army morale and the genuine fear of their soldiers. In populations the great epidemic can cause massive depopulation, economic collapse, religious panic, scapegoating of minorities, and collapse of government authority if enough people die. Politically it can trigger succession crises if rulers die, cause trade routes to be abandoned, strain alliances, and either drive populations toward intense religious devotion or cause them to lose faith entirely.
The sixth type is wound infection, not contagious but responsible for killing more wounded soldiers than the wounds themselves. It infects most wounds without proper treatment. With no surgeon present the majority of wounded soldiers become infected. With a basic surgeon many still become infected, an experienced surgeon reduces this significantly, and an excellent surgeon in clean conditions reduces it to a small fraction. Most infected soldiers die without treatment and most survive with it. Amputation reduces the death rate further but permanently disables the soldier.
The seventh type is a seasonal respiratory illness that peaks in winter and is rarely fatal to healthy soldiers but devastating to weakened armies. It becomes far more lethal when combined with exhaustion, starvation, cold without shelter, or other diseases that are already present in the army.
Disease arrival
Disease spreads through several vectors. When an army carrying disease moves to a new land it carries the disease with it. The chance of transmission per turn varies by disease type and the disease cannot always be detected before the army moves because of the incubation period. Merchants carry disease along trade routes in the same way they carry goods, slower than army spread and impossible to stop without stopping all trade entirely. Disease follows water flow through river systems so downstream lands are vulnerable if upstream is contaminated and flood events guarantee spread downstream. Refugees fleeing disease carry it to their destination creating a dilemma where accepting refugees risks disease but refusing loses goodwill and workers. Large gatherings such as pilgrimages and festivals spread disease through concentrating people from many lands in one place. Actors do not know this and a religious leader organizing a mass gathering during an epidemic believes they are helping people while actually accelerating the spread.
Ships arriving at coastal or river ports can carry unknown diseases from distant regions. The chance of a diseased arrival scales with the trade volume through the port, is higher in summer when more ships travel, and depends on where the ship originates. When disease first arrives nobody knows what it is. The mayor sees that sailors are sick but does not know how serious it is or whether it will spread. The mayor must decide whether to quarantine the ship without knowing if it is necessary. This is the authentic historical dilemma because nobody understood how disease spread. Quarantine applied early is significantly effective but halts trade and angers the population. The mayor must act before the full picture is known. Overland trade caravans arriving from distant regions can carry disease in the same way as ships for lands without port access.
Factors that increase spread include greater population density, winter and other seasons favoring specific disease types, overcrowding, poor water quality, poor sanitation, refugee presence in the land, active trade routes, rainy weather, severe drought which concentrates contamination in remaining water, and flooding which guarantees waterborne outbreak. Factors that decrease spread include population immunity built up through prior exposure, good sanitation infrastructure, a physician present and treating, quarantine in effect, cold weather for most disease types, and snow cover.
Medical personnel
Physicians can be hired via the tavern following the standard hiring system and assigned to an army or to a specific land at an ongoing salary cost. Several types of physician exist. University trained physicians are the most effective, male only in standard historical scenarios, and carry low suspicion of witchcraft. Barber surgeons are practically trained, male in official capacity, good for wound treatment, and carry low suspicion of witchcraft. Apothecaries are licensed sellers of medical preparations, male in official capacity, and carry low to medium suspicion of witchcraft. Folk healers can be either gender, provide community medicine using herbs and traditional knowledge, and carry high suspicion of witchcraft. Midwives are female only, accepted specifically for childbirth, and carry medium to high suspicion of witchcraft if they practice beyond their recognized role. Monastery healers can be both genders but treat patients of their own gender, are sanctioned by the church, and carry very low suspicion of witchcraft. Physicians from minority religious communities carry a suspicion of witchcraft risk that depends on scenario configuration and may be protected at the court level if they serve a powerful patron.
Each physician type reduces mortality for each disease at different rates with wound infection and waterborne diseases seeing the greatest benefit and the great epidemic the smallest since it was nearly untreatable in this era. One physician per roughly two hundred soldiers is effective. Multiple physicians working together provide increasing benefit up to a limit of three physicians after which additional physicians add less. Beyond capacity effectiveness drops.
Medical system configuration
Each scenario configures the medical system independently without any pre-defined regional settings. Whether witch trials exist, whether accusation chains are possible, how the community regards folk healers, what type of accusation mechanism is primary such as court proceedings or poison accusation or community ostracism or tribal elder decision or religious court, the threshold before formal accusation occurs, whether torture is permitted, whether rehabilitation after accusation is possible and through what mechanism, how much gender bias there is in accusations, whether the too effective paradox is in play, how widely accusations spread, whether panic spreads between lands, and whether the game tracks the ground truth of guilt are all set per scenario. Whether a hospital system exists, who funds it, who can access it, whether it is free, and whether it offers advanced specialist care are all configured per scenario. Whether medicine is separated by patient gender, whether female healers are restricted to domestic settings, whether folk healers must frame their practice within the dominant religion to avoid suspicion of witchcraft, whether shamanic healers exist and how visible they are and how protected they are, whether tribal or clan membership replaces formal court processes, whether all medicine must be portable for nomadic scenarios, what multiplier applies to minority physician suspicion of witchcraft risk, what balance exists between poison accusation and witchcraft accusation, and whether animal-based medicine and fermented therapeutic drinks are accepted are all determined by scenario configuration. All names used by these systems including disease names, healer titles, institution names, court names, and event names can be changed per scenario.
Containment measures
Quarantine can be ordered by a mayor, ruler, or noble and can range from complete closure of all entry and exit to restricting only incoming travelers, restricting only trade, or restricting only military movement. Full quarantine is most effective but halts trade and reduces revenue significantly while active. The population reacts with frustration and the order must be actively maintained. It is lifted when disease clears or when political and economic pressure becomes too great.
Water management ordered by a mayor or commander reduces waterborne disease significantly. Options include restricting the army or population to known clean sources, boiling all water which requires fuel, or using wine or beer instead of water. This last option is genuinely effective as actors from this era observe without understanding why and depletes alcohol supplies faster.
Camp sanitation ordered by a commander reduces contact disease spread significantly but costs a turn of reduced movement and requires engineering capability or designated soldiers assigned to the task.
Moving camp ordered by a commander resets the disease level for an army at the cost of a turn of movement. The reasoning available to actors of this era is that the bad air of the infected location must be left behind, which is the miasma theory that happens to be practically correct.
Isolating sick soldiers ordered by a commander or mayor cuts spread within the army significantly. Isolated soldiers cannot fight and some commanders refuse to abandon their sick on moral grounds.
Burning infected areas ordered by mayor or commander reduces the disease level in the land significantly but destroys structures permanently and is used primarily when the great epidemic has taken hold.
Disease as weapon
Wells, fountains, and rivers can be poisoned as described in the main movement and battle requirements, creating guaranteed waterborne disease in that land for a very long duration that requires significant time to clean. Reputation cost is high.
Infected corpses can be hurled into besieged fortifications when the great epidemic or severe disease is present in the attacker's land, creating a disease outbreak inside the fortification. This is historically documented. Reputation cost is very high and military effectiveness in breaking sieges is significant.
Enemy water supplies inside a fortification or camp can be contaminated by a spy or saboteur who gains access. This introduces disease rather than immediate poison, takes longer to show effect, and is harder to detect as deliberate.
A diseased army can be sent through enemy lands intentionally to spread disease to the enemy population. This also spreads disease further within the army itself. Reputation cost is high and the strategic use is to deny territory to the enemy.
Witch and practitioner system
Two fundamentally different situations exist regarding witchcraft accusations. The first is the genuine practitioner who actually performs rituals and believes in their magical abilities. The second is the accused innocent who has done nothing unusual but is named by others due to superstition, personal grudge, or scapegoating.
The genuine practitioner has a level of actual practice ranging from no practice at all through minor charms and fortune telling through active ritual healing to fully dedicated practice, a genuine belief in their abilities, and a level of how visible their practice is ranging from completely secret to widely known in the community. Practice can include herbal preparation combined with ritual, spoken charms and incantations, creation of protective amulets, fortune telling and divination, curse casting, binding rituals, attempts to influence weather, communication with spirits, healing rituals, and keeping specific animals as familiars. Practitioners can be motivated by genuine desire to help, income, community power and status, personal revenge, fulfilling an expected community role, or religious devotion within their own understanding of religion.
Innocent accused people are typically triggered by a patient dying after they visited, living near a location where misfortune occurred, a personal dispute with the accuser, having an unusual appearance, being elderly and living alone, being a foreigner, belonging to the wrong religious minority, owning certain animals, a child dying near them, surviving an epidemic when others died, having predicted something that then happened, being visibly economically successful, or being selected as a scapegoat after a disaster. Accusers are most commonly personal enemies using the charge as a weapon, genuinely frightened neighbors acting in superstitious belief, political rivals, religious authorities consolidating power, investigators looking for cases to justify their position, economic competitors, grieving family members needing an explanation for a death, or accumulated community opinion without any single accuser.
The game tracks whether each accused person is genuinely a practitioner or innocent as ground truth that only the accused themselves knows. No other actor ever has direct access to this truth and all decisions are made based on observation, rumor, superstition, relationships, and political interest. The truth can occasionally surface through skilled investigation by an experienced spy, a rare skeptical judge, or a surviving accused who retracts a forced confession after release. Some scenarios can disable this ground truth tracking entirely for simpler implementation.
The too effective paradox is a genuine feature of the era where successful healing increases witchcraft suspicion rather than reducing it. When the church or physician has given up on a patient who then recovers, when recovery is unusually fast, when a practitioner achieves many consecutive successes, when a high profile patient recovers against expectation, or when a church leader witnesses an apparently impossible recovery, suspicion grows rather than trust. The church leader who declared a patient would die looks wrong and witchcraft becomes the convenient explanation. Being more effective makes a healer more suspicious. This applies to all physician types with folk healers most vulnerable and minority community physicians most of all.
When witch trial systems include torture the accused almost always confesses and that confession names supposed accomplices. Those named are arrested and tortured and in turn name more people creating a self-reinforcing chain. Each person named may be a genuine practitioner or may be completely innocent. The chain continues until a skeptical ruler intervenes, the disease pressure that created the scapegoat need naturally subsides, a genuinely devout religious leader publicly condemns the persecution, or a protector powerful enough to stop it intervenes. The feedback between disease and witch accusation means that practitioners hiding to protect themselves makes disease spread worse which increases the pressure for scapegoats which drives more accusations which causes more practitioners to hide making disease worse still. This historically authentic tragedy emerges from the mechanics without special design.
When an accusation becomes formal a new individual actor is created for the trial if one does not already exist. This actor is created from the population pool on demand and is not created in advance. It has only the minimal attributes needed for the trial including name, gender, age, physical and psychological resilience affecting how long they resist under torture, moral courage affecting whether they maintain innocence, social connections affecting whether witnesses speak for them, wealth affecting whether bribing a judge or witnesses is possible, and whether they are actually a genuine practitioner which only the game itself tracks. When the trial resolves the actor is returned to the population pool if the outcome was quiet and the case closes. If the acquittal was public and notable the actor persists as an individual whose story is known. If executed the actor is deleted after the game processes any chain accusations and martyrdom effects. If imprisoned the actor persists as there may be later release or ransom. If exiled or fled the actor persists in the new location. An anonymised record of every trial outcome is kept regardless so the game can track panic levels, detect accusation patterns, and trigger historical events based on accumulated trials.
New structures required
An apothecary that produces medical supplies from gathered herbs is available in towns only. A healing house that reduces disease mortality in the land requires a physician to be assigned to it and is available in towns only. A more advanced form of healing house accepts patients of all religions, operates on charitable endowment funding, provides its services free of charge, and offers specialized treatments not available in the standard version. A quarantine house at ports reduces the risk of foreign disease arrival. A place where tribal elders give judgement replaces formal court processes in nomadic scenarios. All of these structures can be renamed per scenario.
New goods required
A medical supplies good is produced at apothecaries and monasteries and is consumed when physicians treat disease. Medicinal herbs are gathered from forest and swamp terrain and are used to produce medical supplies. A fermented animal drink produced from horse or cattle herds has both medical and food value, has low alcohol content, is portable, and is available in scenarios where animal-based medicine is culturally accepted. All of these goods can be renamed per scenario.
New actor types required
A shamanic healer whose visibility, community protection, and relationship to the official religion of the land are all configured per scenario can heal, divine, claim to influence weather, and give blessings or curses. This actor is not automatically suspicious of witchcraft in communities where shamanic practice is dominant but faces high accusation risk if monotheist religious authority holds power in the land. A military folk hero in a Cossack or similar military tradition holds the same supernatural reputation as a figure who would be accused of witchcraft in Western European settings but is instead a hero with high community protection and very low accusation risk in their own tradition. Both actor types can be renamed per scenario.
Religion system
The religion system models the role of faith, religious institutions, and clergy in the game world. Religion is both a genuine spiritual force and a political institution. Religious leaders pursue power and wealth using spiritual tools. Truly religious actors exist and operate on different logic from political actors. Multiple religion types are supported including non-monotheistic forms. All names are scenario-configurable and the game is designed to support fictional religions in fictional settings with the possibility of renaming everything to match real historical religions in scenarios that call for it.
Religion types
There are several types of religion structure. The first is a centralized monotheism with a single god, an organized hierarchy from local clergy to a supreme leader at the top, written doctrine, clergy as a formal profession, active conversion of others as a goal, the ability to excommunicate members, the right to crown rulers, religious courts, and all the standard political mechanics of the religion system. The second is a decentralized monotheism with a single god but no central authority where local communities make their own decisions, there is no supreme leader, clergy are less formally organized, reform movements are more likely to emerge, the religion has reduced top-level political role, and local community influence is stronger. The third is an organized polytheism with multiple gods each with specific domains, a temple system, a priestly hierarchy though without a single supreme head, divine favors mechanics where rulers must maintain rituals to keep favor, oracle consultation before major decisions, a sacrifice system, control of sacred sites as a form of religious power, and a natural tolerance of other religions because other gods can be acknowledged as real. The fourth is folk polytheism with multiple spirits and forces rather than named gods, no formal hierarchy, local wise people serving as intermediaries, seasonal rituals, no written doctrine, sacred natural locations such as groves and springs and hills, and no concept of heresy since there is no written standard to violate. The fifth is shamanic religion organized around individual shamans as spirit world intermediaries with no hierarchy, direct personal spiritual contact, no conversion goal, no written doctrine, oral tradition only, and typically existing as a hidden layer beneath an official religion surface. The sixth is animist belief in spirits in all things, purely nature-based with no formal clergy, no conversion goal, minimal institutional role but strong community role. The seventh is syncretic religion which mixes two or more types, most commonly official monotheism over a folk polytheist or shamanic practice that persists in private life while official religion is observed in public. Both sets of mechanics apply at different social levels and the tension between official and folk practice is itself a gameplay element. The degree of integration, how tolerant the official church is of folk practice, and how much of the population privately continues folk practice are all configured per scenario. The eighth is philosophical belief, an ethics or wisdom system requiring no deity, making no supernatural claims, and playing a different political role from the others with focus on wisdom and moral conduct.
Religion configuration
Every religion regardless of type has a name that can be changed in scenario configuration. It has a level of tolerance toward other religions, a setting for whether conversion of others is a goal, a setting for whether afterlife belief exists, a schedule of sacred dates in the calendar, a list of substances that are prohibited to believers such as alcohol or specific foods, rules about gender roles within the faith, a setting for whether one spouse or multiple spouses are permitted, and a setting for whether alcohol is prohibited. Centralized and decentralized monotheisms additionally configure whether a hierarchy exists, the title of the top leader which can be changed, whether excommunication is available, whether the religion has the right to crown rulers, whether religious courts exist, whether forced conversion is permitted, whether clergy must be celibate, and whether reform movements can emerge. Polytheist religions configure a list of deities each with domains and divine favor mechanics, types of priests, whether an oracle exists, whether sacrifice is practiced, types of sacred sites, whether divine approval affects rulers, and whether omens are read before major decisions. Shamanic religions configure the title of the shaman which can be changed, the abilities of shamans, the nature of the spirit world, whether ritual music is required for practice, whether a trance state is part of shamanic work, and whether ancestor veneration plays a role. Syncretic religions configure which official religion forms the surface layer, which folk religion forms the hidden layer, how thoroughly the two have merged, how tolerant the official church is of folk practice, how much of the population privately maintains folk practice, whether old sacred sites have been reused as official holy places, whether old festivals have been renamed as saints days, and whether old gods have been identified with figures from the official religion.
Clergy hierarchy
All religions in the game use the same number of ranks regardless of type but with different titles at each rank configured per scenario. The highest rank is the supreme leader of the religion with the ability to excommunicate, crown rulers, declare holy war, appoint all clergy below them, make formal doctrinal rulings, and call religious councils. This title can be changed in scenario configuration. The next rank is the senior clergy such as cardinals or bishops or their equivalents who run religious courts in their region, promote clergy below them, and exercise political influence in their territory. This title can be changed. Below that is the local clergy who conduct marriages, hear confessions which serve as an intelligence source, exercise local influence, and preside over minor religious courts. This title can be changed. Below that is the leader of a specific monastery who manages its economic output, cannot participate in war, and is subordinate to the religion leader. This title can be changed. The lowest rank is the ordinary monk or travelling monk who gathers intelligence while travelling, attempts conversions, copies manuscripts, and undertakes pilgrimage missions. This title can be changed. All clergy at the lower ranks are represented as population pools rather than individual actors and are only instantiated as individual actors when given a specific mission, accused of heresy, emerging as a reform voice, appointed to a position, or demonstrating genuine holiness.
Political religious leader
The political religious leader pursues wealth and protection of tax exemptions as the highest priority, then political power consolidation, then institutional survival, then suppression of rivals. Spiritual tools serve as instruments of power in this actor type. Excommunication officially saves a sinner's soul but in practice isolates the target politically, releases their subjects from loyalty oaths, and legitimizes military action against them. It works best when the target is already weakened, when grounds exist even if manufactured, and when other rulers can be relied on to enforce the isolation. Used too often it loses credibility and a leader who has depleted its credibility cannot effectively use it as a weapon. Coronation officially provides divine sanction to a ruler but gives the religion leader maximum leverage since conditions can be attached and it can be withheld to support a rival claimant. The strategy of supporting the weaker claimant extracts more concessions from a grateful new ruler. Holy war officially defends the faith but actually expands church influence into conquered territories while nobles fight and die. Declaration of holy war works best when a rival religion is weak or when the internal conflicts of nobles need redirecting outward. The confession network officially provides spiritual reconciliation but every confession heard is a potential intelligence asset. Heresy charges officially enforce doctrine but in practice remove opponents. Used obviously for political purposes they cost the leader credibility. The monastery network officially serves prayer and learning but economically produces goods, geographically covers many lands with loyal educated people, and serves as a land banking system accumulating property over generations. Decisions are driven most urgently by threats to tax exemptions, then by how fast a reform movement is growing, then by how much credibility the excommunication weapon retains, then by succession instability which creates opportunity through coronation leverage, and at all times by the intelligence value flowing through confessions.
Truly religious actor
Genuinely devout actors exist at all levels and in all actor types and this is a personality type rather than a separate actor category. The primary decision logic is based on what is right rather than what serves personal interests. These actors cannot be bribed because they genuinely do not want money. Threats are less effective because acceptance of martyrdom removes fear as a lever. They cannot be politically outmaneuvered because they operate outside the political game entirely. They accumulate authentic popular support because people recognize genuine holiness over time. Their moral authority comes from decades of consistent behavior. Types include the wandering preacher who owns nothing and travels everywhere bypassing church structure to speak directly to people, the reform bishop who holds rank but refuses to play the political game and documents corruption while protecting the poor, the monastery saint who seeks withdrawal from the world but becomes famous despite this, the people protector who stands physically between the powerful and the vulnerable and provides sanctuary for the persecuted, and the educator monk who teaches common people to read and preserves knowledge across generations. These actors publicly denounce corruption, serve the poor directly, refuse corrupt church orders, accept martyrdom when required, build alternative communities, give church wealth away, protect the persecuted, educate common people, bless rulers who are worthy, refuse to bless unworthy rulers, confront corrupt superiors, and support rebellions whose cause is just and whose methods are acceptable. They withdraw support from a rebellion whose methods become unacceptable even if the cause is just, and oppose a new regime that is as corrupt as the old one even after supporting the rebellion that created it. These actors never use spiritual tools for personal power, never use alcohol as a weapon, and cannot be bribed.
Elder covenant religion
The diaspora minority religion has no territorial state and no army of its own. Conversion of others is not a goal of the religion. It typically owns no land. It cannot be the official state religion in most scenarios. It has internal self-governance through its community council which handles internal tax collection, internal dispute resolution, community welfare, and connections between communities in different lands. An internal court handles family law and commercial disputes between community members and religious compliance matters but cannot handle criminal cases or disputes with people outside the community. The community leader title can be changed in scenario configuration. The community council name and the internal court name can also be changed.
The diaspora network connecting communities across many lands provides commercial intelligence from multiple lands simultaneously, the ability to transfer value between communities without physically moving coins through a system of community bonds which is unique to this religion in the game, absorption of refugees from expelled or persecuted communities, and intelligence through family connections across borders. The community bond system can be renamed in scenario configuration.
The community requires explicit protection from a ruler or noble in the form of a charter granting specific rights such as the right to live there, the right to worship, the right to internal governance, the right to trade, the right to lend money at interest, occasionally the right to own property, and the right to maintain a place of worship. Charter obligations always include a higher tax rate than other residents and may include requirements about dress, restrictions on where the community may live, and restrictions on what occupations members may hold. All terms of the charter can be changed in scenario configuration.
The community can be blamed for plagues, crop failures, and economic crises. This risk increases when disasters have recently occurred, when the community's wealth is visibly greater than their neighbors, when the dominant religion is intolerant of minority faiths, and when a demagogue is present to organize sentiment. Strong protection from a powerful patron and genuine affection from the broader population both reduce this risk.
The community leader's primary goal is the survival of the community rather than expansion, conversion, or political power as ends in themselves. Key decisions include negotiating protection charters, making strategic loans to powerful people since a noble who owes the community money is reluctant to expel them, activating the diaspora intelligence network, quietly preparing reserves and emigration plans, lowering the community's visibility when threat increases, seeking new protectors when current ones weaken, offering medical services and translation services to build indispensable relationships, refusing forced conversion, and organizing quiet emigration before expulsion arrives rather than after. This actor never seeks to convert others, never raises an army, and never seeks to become the official religion.
When a community is expelled the expelling power loses access to the credit transfer network, loses the higher tax revenue the community paid, loses skilled workers including craftsmen and physicians, and faces chaos around debts the community held. The destination land that accepts the expelled community gains all of these economic benefits.
In scenarios set in regions where a distinct ancient community exists that differs from the standard form of this religion through rejecting later scriptural additions and following only the original texts, this community can be configured as a sub-type with a different suspicion risk level that may be lower than the standard form in specific contexts. This sub-type can be given a distinct name in scenario configuration.
Ritual goods
Certain goods are consumed in religious ceremonies creating real economic demand and political leverage. Incense is required for major ceremonies and cannot be substituted in high church practice. In northern scenarios it must be imported from southern regions making it a strategic trade dependency and a potential lever for rulers who control those trade routes. Wine is required for the central ceremony of the faith and cannot be substituted. Even in regions that cannot grow grapes the church requires wine creating guaranteed import demand. Specific types of candle made from beeswax rather than tallow are required for ceremonies with tallow candles acceptable as a lower quality substitute. This makes beehives a strategic resource for church supply. Grain is required for the bread used in ceremonies. Oil specifically from olives is required for anointing rituals with other oils acceptable as substitute. At harvest festivals the population gives grain to the church as a form of tithe in goods rather than money. Herbs are used by the official church for blessed preparations and also by folk healers for their own practice. This means the church cannot condemn herbal knowledge without condemning part of its own ritual practice, which creates a tension in scenarios where syncretic religion is active.
A ruler who controls the trade routes supplying the church with goods it needs for ceremonies holds a genuine lever over the church. The church cannot conduct its central rituals without wine and incense and specific candles. However if the ruler actually uses this lever and cuts supply the population notices the disruption to their worship and blames the ruler rather than the church. The leverage is most effective as a threat rather than an action.
Polytheist specific mechanics
When a ruler observes the proper rituals and sacrifices for the relevant deity before a battle or harvest or major decision, the deity grants favor which takes effect as army morale bonuses, harvest blessing, or other practical benefits. When a ruler neglects these rituals divine disfavor creates bad omens before battle, morale penalties, and population fear. Before major decisions rulers and commanders may consult an oracle who gives an ambiguous answer. The actor then interprets this answer according to their personality and beliefs and always believes their interpretation at the time. The ambiguity means the oracle is always technically right after the fact regardless of outcome. Control of specific sacred natural sites such as ancient groves, healing springs, or prominent hills confers religious authority in the surrounding region in the same way that controlling a temple does but through a different mechanism. Destroying a sacred site causes revolt risk and divine disfavor. Converting one to an official monotheist holy place creates syncretic tension. Reading omens from natural events before major decisions is another practice in polytheist traditions where the result is positive or negative and the effect on morale is real because the belief creates real behavior.
Syncretic religion specific mechanics
The fundamental tension in syncretic religion is that the official church persecuting the widespread folk practice of its own population creates revolt risk, while tolerating the practice allows doctrinal drift but keeps the population happier. The chance of witch hunting occurring increases when the official church is intolerant and folk practice is widespread, because there are many potential targets and institutional incentive to prosecute them. When old sacred sites have been physically incorporated as official holy places the physical evidence of the older practice remains visible. When old seasonal festivals have been renamed as saints days the same celebrations continue under new names. When old deities have been identified with official holy figures the population sees continuity in their practice while the church claims successful conversion and both interpretations are correct from within their own frameworks.
Scenario rename keys
Every name in the religion system can be changed in scenario configuration. This includes the names of each religion, all clergy titles at each rank, the name of the community council and internal court of the diaspora minority religion, the name of the community leader, shaman, oracle priest, and temple priest, the names of any deities in polytheist scenarios, the names of goods used in ritual including the community bond and incense and ritual candle and sacred oil, and the names of concepts including divine favor, excommunication, holy war, the practice of selling indulgences, and the term for sacred sites.
Nobles
There should be nobles of different nationalities in the lands, approximately 5% of population, all the rulers of the countries should be nobles and could be either men or women. Some of the nobles should be favorites of the ruler of the country. Nobles could be either men or women. Nobles are the main force of the armies. Nobles in the army should be paid by the commander of the army once per 60 turns; if the army is the army of the ruler, the ruler should pay them salary once per 60 turns. The ruler of the country should not pay to the nobles that are not part of his army. Nobles should have a level of respect from other nobility and from the population, should have some level of fear of them, should be included in politics and war. Weapons should be bought by them for themselves and for their soldiers if they have them except mercenaries. High level nobles may have their army and should support it if they have it. Nobles should have different levels of thirst of power, strength, dignity, caution, superstition, beauty, culture and religiosity, some of them should not accept bribes, betrayal, should not execute captives, others are ok with it. Preferred way of travelling for high level nobility is in carriages, when in battle – mounted. All nobles may have families that are also included in politics. Women nobles should not be the part of the army, but higher rank women nobles could (not must) participate directly in battles as commanders if they are strong and nobody else could be nominated, otherwise they nominate leadership to their husband or their noble subordinate. Subordinate nobles may (but not must) be rewarded by land or the next title for their service. In this case the land could be given by the master noble, the title could be granted only by the ruler of the country and the master could ask the ruler to give the title to this noble. The higher title nobles could be leaders of armies or units. The lowest level nobles that start their career should be ordinary soldiers and should not have any characteristics except nationality and religion and land of origin due to performance restrictions and should get them when promoted to the next title - this could happen if they are brave in battle and did remarkable impact on result. In such cases the ruler could promote some of them with further titles. For women nobles of the lowest title granting next title is the part of the marriage to noble of higher title. 
Granting nobility to ordinary people and titles system 
Rulers can grant nobility to a person if they require a new noble, but these nobles would not be respected by true nobles. Same for bastards. Bastards have more thirst for power. Granting nobility requires assignment of the lowest title and may be (but not mandatory) rewarded by land or structures. Titles have some sequence that ends with final. Some titles do not have a sequence and are granted as a reward. Noble of title cannot be subordinate to noble of lower or equal title. If title requires it, nobles become subordinate directly to rulers of the country. 
Prestige of the noble
The higher noble’s prestige increases if they are part of the country’s ruler’s court. To be part of the court of the ruler, the noble should spend money on unique carriages, furniture, clothes, tableware, should participate all the balls that are organized by the ruler of the country and should be accepted by the ruler. Spending money requires more tax collection and more usage of resources of the owned lands. The ruler’s prestige increases if his court is bigger. Also, higher nobles’ prestige increases if they are nominated for one of the positions like chancellor, coin holder, defense commander.
Dignity defense system
There should be a system of defending the dignity of the nobles and their families. It could include duels (preferred for men) or trying to kill the offender via help of assassin (preferred by women) or asking the court for help (lowest priority). The duel must happen or the noble that was called for a duel gets ashamed in case the caller of the duel is the same title or higher as the noble that was called. The duel could happen (but not mandatory) in case the caller for the duel has lower title and could result in using assassin to kill the caller if he is too rude. If one of the participants is woman, she should nominate the defender and if nobody found she should reject the duel and hiring of assassin could be done. Duels may be banned by the ruler of the country, but they will still take place secretly with the consequence that if the duelists are caught, they may be suspected of attempted murder with further consequences. An attempted murder could be committed if someone blackmailed a noble. If the ruler of the country is offended by his subordinate, he could order to jail or behead the offender. If the ruler of the country is offended by the noble that belong to other country, he asks other country’s leader to punish that noble and if he does not punish the war could begin. If the offender is the rule of other country, the war should begin.
Relationships and love system
If a noble or a priest loves someone, they may give their beloved gifts, use the services of bards, and invite their beloved to a ball, hunt, or festival. The level of success depends on the noble/priest person's beauty and the amount of attention they give their beloved, as well as whether the person is married. If a person is married and accepts love, this could be a reason for a duel or assassination if discovered. 
Audience with the noble (including ruler of the country)
There should be a possibility of asking the noble for audience where some messages could be shared or some proposals could be conducted, or some requests could be made. The noble could accept or reject the audience request and could react differently to these messages, proposals and requests that are shared with him while the audience. There could be an attempt to murder while the audience with different levels of success depending on the number of people in the room.
Hiring different actors in the game
To hire spies, assassins, tax collectors, recruiting agents, merchants, the player could visit the tavern where he could find all these actors that are in this land and could (not must) pay in advance for their job. The player could give them orders after hiring. Or the player could make an order to chancellor to hire spies or assassins, coin holder to hire tax collectors or merchants, defense commander to hire recruiting agents. The player should give them money for this hiring. There could be an attempt to murder with different levels of success depending on the availability of hired guards if the player is the target victim of the assassin.
Messaging
Messages are the core mechanics of the game and could be done via letters or in oral form. All the information should be taken from the messages and should be interpreted by the actors, no total information should be used and collected in the game.  No decision by any actors in the game should be taken from information that is not part of the messages that were received by them. Messages could contain orders to collect taxes, orders to collect/disband the army, orders to sell/buy some goods or resources, orders to the already existing army units to attack the land or to defend the land, information about lands like number of forces there, level of loyalty, amount of goods and their prices, order to the spy to check loyalty of the noble (could be done via observing or via interception of his/her correspondence) or to organize rebellion in the land, offers of war and peace with details like marriage proposals if accepted, calls for a duel, proposals to betray the sovereign for some reward, proposals to join the alliance of nobles to get the throne, proposals to get lands of the noble if supports the alliance of nobles to get the throne, orders to join the council, orders with nomination of recipient to chancellor or coin holder or defense commander, proposals to join a ball or a festival or a hunt, orders to print the coins. Letters could be protected by stamps that could be broken and restamped with some level of accuracy by the spies that can read the letter and if needed change the content. Letters could be sent via messengers and doves and should be kept or destroyed. If the letter is kept for further usage, it could be stolen (with some probability depending on the guard level) by the opponent using a spy. Letters could be intercepted and could be used in politics like Casus Belli, decrease of loyalty etc. Level of chance of interception is higher if the message is sent through the hostile lands or there is a spy with the order to intercept the correspondence, or messenger is bribed or killed. Oral messages require messenger and could be revealed to enemies of the noble if the messenger is not loyal or the message is against the family of the messenger or the messenger himself. Both letters and oral messages could be fake. Messages could be done by monks, priests including religion leaders, spies, assassins, army commanders, unit commanders, nobles including rulers of the countries, merchants, mayors, ambassadors, scouts, recruiting agents, bards, all of them could be used as messengers. There could be also messengers from post offices, these could be bribed to reveal the letter of the message. The post office provides up to 20 messengers and horses for them. When the horse is tired it could be changed for the fresh one from the post office by paying money. Also, the post office should give horses to royal messengers by request. The messenger has a target location, which is told to him by the sender where the recipient should be, and if the messenger arrived there and did not find the recipient, he could, depending on the senders' orders, either wait or move on to the next known location, which is told by people from the current land if the recipient did not leave at night, until he finds the recipient. Doves are sent to a specific location and if the recipient is not found there, they do not continue searching for the recipient. Any message should include "Deliver personally", "Via messenger", "Via dove" and should default to the "Via messenger" even if it is the same land for the sender and recipient. All deliveries inside 1 land should take 1 turn except the contact personally but it has to be limited to up to 10 visits per turn and if this in-person delivery of the message is to the person the title of which is lower than his own this visit lowers his (visitor) prestige by creating a corresponding gossip except the visit to the chancellor, coin holder or defense commander but if the title of the noble is equal or higher it must not impact. Spy system
The spy system covers intelligence gathering, identity deception, message interception, and covert operations. All intelligence has an age and a confidence level and decays in usefulness over time. No actor in the game has complete knowledge of the world and all decisions must be based on received messages and gathered intelligence. Cover identities require active maintenance rather than persisting automatically. The risk of being detected is real and is tracked through each interaction a spy has while undercover. All actor titles and mission names can be changed in scenario configuration.
Spy actor
Spies can be hired via the tavern following the standard hiring system or appointed by the chancellor. They can be of any gender and any social background. The skills most relevant to spy work are intelligence gathering, deception, forgery, lockpicking, knowledge of poisons, ability to maintain disguises, and ability to build and manage networks of informants. Personality matters significantly with emotional detachment growing through experience making a spy more effective at their work but less human in their relationships over time. Resilience affects how long a spy can withstand torture. Caution and loyalty to handler and employer shape decisions throughout. The same actor type covers both intelligence gathering and assassination work since many operatives do both and the distinction lies in which skills and missions are emphasized. There are also handlers who manage networks of other operatives, courier specialists who carry messages, and double agents who work for two sides simultaneously.
Intelligence types
All intelligence the game tracks has a description of what was learned, a confidence level, an age measured in turns that grows each turn and reduces effective confidence, an identified source, and a reliability rating for that source. Land intelligence covers estimates of population satisfaction, garrison strength, supply levels, fortification condition, noble presence or absence, and disease level. Army intelligence covers estimated size expressed as a range rather than an exact figure, last known position, observed direction of movement, commander identity, estimated supply state, and estimated morale. Noble intelligence covers location, current plans, relationships, secrets with potential use for blackmail, financial condition, and loyalty to their sovereign. Military intelligence covers troop movements, fortification weaknesses, supply routes, and battle plans. Economic intelligence covers information about trade routes, prices in distant markets, and convoy schedules. Personal intelligence covers affairs and secrets, violations of religious rules, financial crimes, personal relationships and who a person loves, and material that could be used for blackmail.
Intelligence decay
All intelligence grows older each turn and effective confidence falls as age increases. In the first few turns there is no decay. After that it decays slowly for a while then more rapidly. Very old intelligence is nearly worthless in terms of confidence. Different types of information decay at different rates. Land supply levels and army positions change quickly and their intelligence decays fast. A noble's secrets are about past events that do not change and decay slowly. The state of fortifications changes slowly and their intelligence decays very slowly. Relationship information decays at a moderate rate. All the situation vectors that actors use when making decisions are derived from the actor's knowledge rather than from the actual state of the world. A commander with intelligence that is many turns old treats it as near worthless and scouts before acting on it. This creates authentic fog of war where the gap between what actors know and what is actually true is itself a gameplay element.
Spy missions
Gathering land intelligence requires travelling to the land and observing for several turns producing a package of intelligence about that land. Gathering army intelligence requires observing the enemy army specifically and produces estimates of size, position, and condition. Risk is higher when close to an active army. Intercepting correspondence requires a spy to be stationed on the message route or to have bribed the messenger. When a letter is intercepted the spy can read it and reseal it so the message continues slightly delayed, read and modify the content and reseal it where forgery skill determines how detectable the tampering is, read it and destroy it so the message never arrives and the absence is eventually noticed, or pass it directly to the handler stopping the message entirely which risks a diplomatic incident if the message was official. Forging a letter creates a false message that appears to come from a real sender. Quality depends on knowledge of how the sender writes, access to their seal, and forgery skill. Uses include false military orders, love messages designed as a trap, false political proposals, fake death certificates, and manufactured evidence. Planting false information introduces a false story into the enemy's intelligence network and requires access to their information channels. Bribing an official turns an enemy authority figure into a regular intelligence source and creates an ongoing relationship at the risk that the official may be working as a double agent. Organizing a rebellion in a target land requires a spy already present there and provides initial weapons so the rebellion has a chance of seizing an arsenal. Assassination requires physical access to the target, uses methods that vary from poisoning to arranged accidents to direct attack, and has a success probability that depends on the target's guards and caution. Finding enemy spies in own lands requires a network of informants and produces identified enemy operatives. Extracting a captured friendly actor requires getting access to where they are held and bringing them out. Economic sabotage destroys supply stores, contaminates water, or burns harvests and requires physical access to the target.
Cover identity
The depth of a cover ranges along a spectrum. At one end is true identity with no deception, maximum trust from anyone who knows the spy, and maximum recognition risk. A step inward is partial concealment where specific facts are hidden using selective truth. This is the easiest to maintain. A prepared false persona with a consistent invented background, false name and origin, and maintained for the duration of a specific mission is the standard form of spy tradecraft. Deeper still is a long-term embedded identity where the community has accepted the spy as the person they claim to be and genuine relationships have been built under that cover. At the far end is permanent identity abandonment where the original person is considered dead or simply vanished and the spy lives permanently as someone else.
A cover identity has a name, an occupation claim that should be plausible given the spy's actual skills, a claimed place of origin which being distant makes it harder to verify, possibly a claimed religion that may differ from the real one, a claimed social status, an invented history, a depth level, any supporting documents, optional physical disguise, a list of people who have met this cover identity, a record of whether it has been compromised, and a lifecycle state from prepared through active to dormant or compromised to blown or cleanly retired.
The quality of a cover depends on whether the claimed occupation matches skills the spy actually has so they can answer detailed questions naturally, whether they genuinely know the claimed place of origin, whether they know the claimed religion's practices well enough, how large a gap there is between the claimed social status and the real one since mannerisms betray real status, whether supporting documents exist, and the quality of any physical disguise. Some situations provide natural cover bonuses: plague in the land where bodies are burned and records are incomplete, recent battle where the chaos means missing persons are normal.
Vectors by which a cover can be detected include a previous meeting between the observer and the spy in real life, gaps in the spy's knowledge of their claimed background that appear when asked specific questions, habit slippage where real training and mannerisms emerge under stress or familiarity or the influence of alcohol, an observer using their own contacts to verify the cover story, the spy being unable to produce documents when asked, wrong responses in a religious ceremony if a different religion has been claimed, and an accent inconsistent with the claimed place of origin. A cover that has been used and then rested can be reused in different regions making it a valuable asset worth protecting. A blown cover can never be used again in that region.
A spy choosing which identity to use considers whether the target already knows them personally in which case a real trusted relationship may extract more information than a false one, whether they are already known in this particular land in which case a cover is required, whether the mission requires specific access that only a particular type of cover can provide, and whether the risk level is low enough that no cover is needed.
Cover documents
The most common document is a travel pass required to cross between some lands, issued by a ruler, mayor, or military commander. A guild membership certificate proves craft or trade affiliation and a genuine one obtained by actually joining the guild is the best option since a forgery risks exposure if someone contacts the guild. A letter of introduction vouches for the bearer and a forgery risks exposure if someone contacts the apparent author. A religious travel document such as a pilgrim's certificate or a letter from one monastery to another is easy to obtain genuinely and opens many doors in religious society. A merchant's license to trade in specific lands provides a plausible reason to be anywhere. A military discharge certificate explains particular skills and extensive travel history. A physician's license gives access to sickrooms which are intelligence opportunities. A death certificate proving someone has died is specifically useful for fake death operations. A document claiming noble title carries the highest scrutiny and is almost never successfully forged.
Document quality is perfect for a genuinely obtained document. Genuine documents obtained through bribery or relationship carry a small irregularity risk. Forged documents have quality equal to the forger's skill minus the difficulty of forging that particular document type. Stolen documents from a living person are at risk if the theft is reported. Documents taken from a dead person are safe if the death was private but risky if it was public since authorities know the holder is dead. Checks on documents range from a guard's glance which only catches obvious problems, through a careful official examination that checks whether the story behind the document is consistent, to expert investigation by a spy hunter or inquisitor that checks the document against original records. An expert check may require contacting the issuing authority but this takes time which may work in the spy's favor and is impossible if the land that issued it is an enemy.
Fake death
A spy, fugitive, debtor, or anyone with strong reason to disappear from their current life may attempt to fake their death. A rumor simply spread by word of mouth is the lowest quality and cheapest option with no physical evidence. A staged accident with witnesses is more convincing. A false burial where something is buried and a priest records the death is the most convincing fixed option and requires a complicit priest. Disappearing during a battle where no body is expected to be recovered is often the cheapest and most believable option since missing persons in battle are normal. A physician reporting the death is very convincing especially during a plague where no body is available because it was burned for safety. Entering a monastery and dying to the secular world is a legally clean and accepted form of identity change that prevents return to secular life without scandal.
The quality of a fake death depends on the method used, whether there is an official death record from a priest or physician, whether multiple witnesses saw the death or its aftermath, whether there is a body or grave, and whether a plague or battle provided natural cover. People who helped with the deception are vulnerabilities since they know the truth.
Each person in the world who might be looking for this individual assesses the fake death separately. A trusted close friend is more skeptical than a bitter enemy. An experienced investigator is more skeptical than a naive one. Someone who interacted with the subject very recently is more skeptical than someone who last saw them long ago. Someone actively investigating is harder to fool than someone passively accepting news.
For archived actors who have faked their death the game stores a single quality score calculated at the time of the deception and a short list of specific vulnerabilities rather than processing the full cover machinery each turn. The quality score degrades very slowly over time as memories fade and records decay. When someone actively searches for the person the game calculates a single probability of the truth being found based on the current quality score, the capability and method of the searcher, and which specific vulnerabilities are accessible to them. If the searcher is using torture on someone who helped with the deception the chance of discovery is very high. If the searcher can access official records from the place the death was supposedly documented the chance is significant. If the searcher has agents in the area where the person is now living the chance scales with how well those agents are embedded. This calculation is only performed when an active search occurs and the archive record is not processed otherwise. Processing stops entirely when all parties who might be looking have come to believe the death and no one with the resources to act on doubt remains active.
New identity
A new identity can be used as part of a fake death operation for permanent escape or as part of spy tradecraft without any fake death, for instance when an operative needs to approach a specific target without being recognized. A new identity has a new name, a claimed place of origin chosen to be difficult to verify, a claimed occupation, optionally a claimed religion, a measure of how different it is from the original identity ranging from same person different name to completely different persona, optional physical changes to appearance, whether forged documents support the identity, and whether people in the new location have been cultivated who accept this person as who they claim to be. The risk of discovery comes from old habits such as skills not matching the claimed occupation, regional accent from the wrong place, wrong religious customs, or bearing inconsistent with the claimed background. Being seen by someone who knew the original identity in a place where the new one is being used creates risk. Living in an area with active intelligence networks creates risk. An active investigation targeting specifically this person creates the highest risk. All of these risks decrease over time as people forget, with greater physical distance from the old life, with deliberate avoidance of former contacts, and by building deeper roots in the new community and life. The gamble of a new identity is that discovery means facing the original consequences plus additional consequences for the deception making the total outcome worse than whatever was being escaped.
Letter interception and forgery
A spy on a message route can intercept letters and choose to read and reseal with no visible sign of tampering if their skill is sufficient, read and alter the content and reseal where the quality of resealing determines whether the recipient is suspicious, read and destroy so the message never arrives where the absence is eventually noticed, or pass it to the handler stopping the message entirely at the risk of a diplomatic incident if the message was official. When a seal is broken and resealed the quality of the work determines whether the recipient notices damage. A suspicious recipient may verify the content with the sender which exposes the interception. Forging a message entirely to appear from a specific known sender requires knowledge of how that sender writes, access to their seal, and sufficient skill. The most dangerous forgery is a message to a target purportedly from someone the target loves designed to lure them to a specific location for an ambush. This requires the highest quality since the target knows the beloved personally and will notice anything inconsistent with their normal communication.
Spy network
A spy network has a handler who manages operations, individual spies working in the field, and informants who pass along information without being full operatives. The network has a coverage level in each land, an overall reliability reflecting how trustworthy the sources are, a depth level reflecting how embedded the assets are, and a secrecy level reflecting how unknown the network is to enemies. Building a network involves placing spies in key lands, bribing local officials to create insider sources, recruiting local informants who are cheaper than full operatives, and establishing professional covers that create natural information flows. Maintaining a network costs money each turn and assets may be captured or turned by enemies and coverage degrades without active attention. Finding enemy spies in one's own lands involves watching for suspicious behavior, interrogating suspects, turning captured assets to work the other way, and monitoring message traffic. A double agent is an enemy spy who has been turned to work for the other side, providing insight into the enemy network and the ability to feed false information in, always with the underlying risk that the agent is actually still working the other side making a third layer of deception possible.
Spy decisions
When a spy decides what to do in a given situation their personality shapes the outcome strongly. A cold professional has a high threshold for aborting a mission and a low threshold for eliminating witnesses. A nervous beginner underestimates how exposed they are and overestimates their own capability. A veteran made paranoid by close calls weights every risk higher than average which is a justified response to experience but can cause missed opportunities. A spy who has recently had a near escape treats their current exposure level as even more dangerous than it appears. A spy whose emotional detachment has dropped too low through accumulated moral conflict may find that their conscience overrides their training. A spy whose identity as a cover has come to know who they really are faces a specific vulnerability that an enemy can exploit. All of these factors drive whether a spy presses ahead with a mission, gathers more information first, extracts immediately, goes silent and waits, or in extreme circumstances defects entirely.
Scenario rename keys
All actor titles including spy, assassin, handler, informant, and double agent can be changed in scenario configuration. All mission type names can be changed. All document type names can be changed. All cover identity level names can be changed. All operation type names including fake death, identity change, network building, and finding enemy operatives can be changed.
Politics and succession rules 
Politics should be marriage-based, succession should be based on the rights of elders and the possibility to disagree and start war or rebellion if so. The ruler of the country should be ordained by the leader of the religion that is official in the country and should have a symbol of his power (crown etc.). The ruler of the country could use either his or his subordinate noble family for politics and this could be either the factor of increase or decrease of loyalty of the given noble or his family that could disagree due to different view on the marriage of his family members or not enough beauty of the candidate and if loyalty is too low start revolt or sabotage in some way. There could be possibilities of hiring the assassin to poison or kill the opponent in other way (success depends on level of guarding and level of cautious of the target). If the assassin was not paid, the hirer becomes the target of this assassin. If assassin fails, there is a chance of his arrest or death. The official diplomacy should be done based on ambassadors that should reach the land of the corresponding noble for whom the official proposal is done. The answer could be sent by dove from ambassador or by himself in person or left w/o any answer if ambassador is imprisoned/killed. The ambassador could be appointed among the messengers. The unofficial politics could be done via messages sent by messengers or doves. 
Move rules
1 turn switches night to day or vice versa. Usually everybody rests and does not move at night but there could be exceptions (such as moving at night to avoid combat during a retreat, etc.). Nighttime gives + to stealth. All movements is done via list of standard routes that have the list of lands they cover (like route 1 has start point at land A, ends at land Z requires to go through the land B, land C, land D) and are used by actors while moving in the next way: move to land A using route 1, then move to land B via route 2 etc. until the target land is reached. There could be also secret shorter, more optimized routes for part of the lands that could be revealed to the actors by locals if they are loyal and got paid for that. The secret routes could also be a trap and end up in swamps with a 10% probability of losing part of the army and supplies or in the ambush of the local bandits if the population is hostile. Resources and money and goods except artillery could be transferred between lands via convoys of wagons that could be guarded by armies of different sizes. Artillery could be transferred using primarily a horse or a cow in exception cases. 1 wagon could transfer up to 1000 volume-weight and can hold up to 2 light artilleries. 1 wagon needs 2 horses or cows and 1 person to ride. 1 horse/cow can transport up to 200 volume-weight on it. 1 boat could transfer up to 1000 volume-weight. Speed on foot is 1 land to land per turn. Artillery speed = speed on foot. Speed with horse = 2 lands in galloping mode and 1 land in ordinary mode per turn. A horse in galloping mode can’t ride more than 1 turn. Speed of boat = speed on foot. Speed of wagon or carriage or expensive carriage = speed on foot. Moving 2 turns in a row leads to tired state and requires 1 turn to rest to return to normal state, moving 3 turns in a row leads to exhaustion and needs 1 turn to rest to return to normal state. Moving 6 days in a row w/o rest force to rest anyway. Lack of food or water leads to exhaustion that cannot be solved via rest but only via eating food and drinking water. When the army or convoy of wagons or herd or other actors (spies, nobles, assassins, recruiting agents, monks, scouts, bards) move they should check if there is a not drained river in the land where they are moving to. If there is a river all actors like spies, nobles, assassins, recruiting agents, monks, scouts, bards are grouped in the units to cross the river. After that the partial move is performed. If there is a river ford/crossing the move of up to 20 units/herds/convoys/carriages per turn is performed.  If there is a bridge/dam the move of up to 40 units/herds/convoys/carriages per turn is performed. If there are only boats available, the move of up to 1 unit/herd per 1 boat available is performed. If there is a river and no boats there, the move could be done (but it’s up to the actor that moves) with 80% chance to drown while crossing. In case the army cannot cross the river in 1 turn, the army divides into 2 until all army moves to the target location. If an army is crossing a river, then the top priority should be given to the crossing of the higher nobility and their property, followed by army units or army convoys; other actors move only after the army has crossed the river. If the capacity of the crossing/bridge/dam/ford/boats available is not enough for all moving actors, maximum actors move and the rest of actors that have not moved yet wait for another turn to try to cross the river.
Consuming supplies mechanics
1 citizen, 1 actor (noble, monk, spy, assassin etc.), 1 soldier, 1 horse and other domestic animals needs 1 food satiety and 1 water per 2 turns, they could die with 10 % chance after 10 turns if not have food or water. Turns to die from famine = 72 turns, from thirst = 14 turns. Hunger and thirst turn the condition into exhaustion, which cannot be relieved by rest, but only by eating and drinking. Spies, monks, assassins, scouts, merchants, recruiting agents, bards, mayors can take food automatically if they are in the village/town with enough food and water. Soldiers can get food from citizens or convoys that do not belong to their army or from herds, but this will decrease the satisfaction level of the origin/target lands of these convoys/herds and if there is famine or thirst it could lead to revolt of the origin/target lands of these convoys/herds when news reaches them. Food need is satisfied for horses and other animals automatically if the end of move is on pastureland and it is not winter. Water need is automatically satisfied for animals if the end of the turn is on the water containing terrain. The mood of the people decreases if they eat animal food. Usage of alcohol lowers caution levels to 50% and increases mood if soldiers do not eat animal food. The army can take food that could be transported by soldiers, and water without convoys for 4 turns. Each actor (noble, monk, spy, assassin etc.) has a flask that does not need to be bought and gives water per 4 turns. Water in flasks and in barrels could be refilled if there is a well or fountain or river in the location where the army stays. If water is poisoned there is a 50% chance of death for both humans and animals each turn, they drink poisoned water. The crops should be cooked before being eaten by humans, and this requires cauldrons, fireplaces and some other resources like salt, water, fish and meat. The meat and fish could be dried (which requires 14 turns) or should be cooked. Some of the food satisfies both food and water needs. There is some food that is served to animals. Food that does not need the cauldron for cooking could be transported by soldiers w/o convoys. Food that is cooked in cauldrons should be cooked and eaten immediately; others could be stored. The food receipts are the next ones. 1 piece of porridge requires 1 piece of crops and 1 piece of water and 1 piece of salt and needs a cauldron with fireplace for cooking and gives 5 points of food satiety. Porridge with meat or fish requires 1 piece of crops and 1 piece of meat or fish and 1 piece of water and 1 piece of salt and needs a cauldron with fireplace for cooking and gives 10 points of food satiety. 1 piece of soup needs 1 piece of water and 1 piece of meat or fish and 1 piece of salt and needs a cauldron with fireplace for cooking and gives 4 points of food satiety and 1 point of water satiety. If these food recipes are cooked without salt, they are considered not tasty (lowers mood) but keep the same level of food and water satieties. 1 piece of grapes gives 1 food satiety. 1 piece of bread gives 4 food satiety. 1 piece of cheese gives 3 food satiety. 1 piece of milk or water or beer or wine gives 1 water satiety. 1 dried meat or 1 dried fish gives 2 points of food satiety. 1 piece of straw is animal food and gives 1 point of food satiety. 1 piece of uncooked crops could be eaten by animals and gives 1 food satiety.
Economics and Trade
Money could be made via printing coins and is done in the capital by mint if the order to print coins is received. Coins are made from silver or gold, a piece of silver or gold results in 10000 silver or gold coins correspondingly. Making money in different places is considered a crime. Coins should be collected via taxes from owned lands; they are collected periodically. Taxes could be also gathered from trade – each deal gives some tax. In some cases, coins could be offered by actors like spies, nobles, assassins, recruiting agents, monks, scouts, bards as a present or could be part of offerings in negotiations. The present increases the loyalty of the ruler to the actor that did the present. If taxes are too high for the population, it could plan a revolt. If taxes on trade are too extensive the black market grows. Money is local and should be transferred via convoys of wagons between the lands in the place where it should be used. There is no global money for the actors in the game. Trade is performed via merchants that sell their goods and buy needed goods or resources in the lands or overseas territories. Overseas trade gives all kinds of resources but needs ships to perform such trade. 1 ship carries up to 20000 volume-weight. Lands produce typically specific resources and goods for sale and require other resources/goods for themselves. All trade is done on the several local markets, and on the periodical markets (these are huge). There are lists of standard trade routes that cover the list of markets involved and what goods in what numbers are to be sold and to be bought on each. Merchants are assigned to these standard routes. The player can hire the merchant to sell/buy the goods/resources for him. Merchants use wagons to transport their goods and money. The local market of the land cannot provide unlimited amounts of desired product and cannot hold more than some limit. The market of the land does not sell the product if the amount of the product is low and is critical for the survival of the land. Nobles can gather periodically taxes also for themselves from owned lands. Taxes could be gathered in resources, goods or money. Trade route stops if there is a war in one of the lands the market of this route is located, or in case there is a war between countries, and the route includes markets of these countries. 
Battles and battlefields
Army requires resources and goods that are transported with the army via convoys of wagons or could be transported in little amounts by the army for short term campaigns. Army contains several units that are led by nobles or other actors. Units contain soldiers of different types, and each soldier is an ordinary man or noble of specific nationality and religion and land of origin. Army of non-nobles could be collected for garrison service or as auxiliary units or could arrive due to rebellion. Garrison service armies fulfil also police functions. Units of non-nobles typically are less effective and have less morale in battle than units of nobles. Units of nobles may be mixed with non-nobles who are their servants. The unit strength is a maximum of 40 soldiers. If there is artillery in the army it should be a part of some unit/units. If there are some convoys in the army, they should be a part of some unit/units. Units could be gathered via conscription of the ordinary men population or nobles via recruiting agents (in this case leader of the army should give them corresponding weapons and goods) or via hiring mercenaries via recruiting agents (limited amounts in the land, appear from experienced soldiers after demobilization). Types of soldiers are pikeman without armor (needs 1 pike), light pikeman (needs 1 pike and 1 light armor), pikeman (needs 1 pike and 1 armor), pikeman with saber without armor (needs 1 pike and 1 saber), light pikeman with saber (needs 1 pike, 1 saber and1 light armor), pikeman with saber (needs 1 pike, 1 saber and 1 armor), musketeer (needs 1 gun and 1 saber), musketeer without saber (needs 1 gun), armored musketeer without saber (needs 1 gun and 1 light armor), armored musketeer (requires 1 gun, 1 saber and 1 light armor), swordsman (requires 1 saber), swordsman with shield (requires 1 saber and 1 shield), armored swordsman (requires 1 saber and 1 light armor), heavy armored swordsman (requires 1 saber and 1 armor), armored swordsman with shield (needs 1 saber, 1 light armor and 1 shield), heavy armored swordsman with shield (needs 1 saber, 1 armor and 1 shield), archer (required 1 bow), archer with saber (required 1 bow and 1 saber), archer with shield (needs 1 bow and 1 shield and 1 saber), armored archer (needs 1 bow and 1 light armor), armored archer with saber (needs 1 bow, 1 saber and 1 light armor), militia (needs 1 war scythe), light armored militia (needs 1 war scythe and 1 light armor), armored militia (needs 1 war scythe and 1 armor), peasants (needs 1 scythe or 1 tool or 1 agricultural tool or 1 axe), light armored peasants (needs 1 light armor and needs 1 scythe or 1 tool or 1 agricultural tool or 1 axe), armored peasants (needs 1 armor and needs 1 scythe or 1 tool or 1 agricultural tool or 1 axe), engineers (requires 1 tool). All of these soldiers could be mounted. All these soldier types could be named differently in different countries. All unarmored soldiers are vulnerable to guns and bows volleys and are weaker than armored soldier types in close combat. Light armor and shields give more advantages in close combat and some levels of protection from bows volley; armor gives some level of protection from guns and bows volleys and gives more advantage in close combat. Pikes and war scythes are more effective in close combat against cavalry and in close combat without entering the enemy ranks. Cavalry attacks can break ranks of enemy infantry without pikes or battle scythes and have a significant impact on morale. Cavalry attacks of soldiers with pikes could break ranks of enemy infantry more effectively. Guns and bows volley could kill horses. Light artillery volley impacts morale and could be used for firing light shells into units. Heavy artillery volley impacts morale significantly and could be used for firing heavy shells into units or bombarding structures. Chance of revolt grows if the message about revolt in the land appears in the army unit with soldiers from that land. If the army unit contains citizens of the revolted land, there is high possibility of revolting in the army unit if it is sent to fight against the rebellion. Mercenaries could change sides or retreat if bribed. Some percent of mercenaries become bandits (consider as an army) after the end of contract if no war occurs and they are not paid enough. Bandits attack convoys if strong enough and hide in the land avoiding battles if forces are stronger than bandit army. Bandits could be exterminated via finding them via scouting and winning the battle against them. Battles are performed on the lands between armies that are on the same land and could last several turns. Battles could be avoided if the army is in stealthy mode and is not big enough. Battles could be conducted either by the player or by nobles or by another actor that is delegated there as a general. Armies in tired state fight 20% less effective than in normal state, armies in exhausted state fight 50 % less effective than in normal state. If one unit runs away there is a high chance of breaking the ranks and retreat of nearby units. The battle must be conducted by each army with a selected sequence of tactics (up to 10 per 1 turn), available in the list studied by the commander, and in battle the sequence of tactics or the tactics themselves can be changed as needed and possible. The change of the tactics could be done, if possible, via messenger to the commander of the unit or via flag for specific unit (if it was agreed before the battle) or via horn for everybody. Horns and flag signals cannot be heard/seen for units that are in close combat. List of tactics depends on terrain. Tactics could use obstacles. There are also commands like charge, retreat that is done via horn to everybody or to specific units (if it was agreed before the battle). Generals could be taught some tactics in military schools or copied from the enemy if observed it. Ambush is one of the tactics and success rates depending on terrain, size of the army, success of the enemy scouts and weather. There should be flags for each unit, losing the flag leads to demoralization. Some flags belong to such famous units, that they make fear in opponents if they observe one. The flags may have their own name, e.g. the royal St. Paul’s flag etc. Army should have morale based on commander experience/respect, amount of food, last battle experience (won/lost/retreat), number of losses, balance of army power with enemy. Army has demoralization if the commander is killed or ran away and has morale boost if commander joins the ranks.  If morale is too low desertion could happen. Participation in the battle increases popularity of the commander if there is a victory, execution of captives increases fear of commander and leads to different reactions from religion leaders. Defeat leads to fleeing of part of the defeated army that becomes bandits. Winner can take a hostage from captured nobles and set a ransom for freeing or keep them imprisoned or execute them or set them free. This impacts fear and popularity. One of the possible battle options is in case there are some fortifications with garrison. Fortification could be (but not guaranteed) stormed successfully with superiority 3 to 1 or higher if same or lower level of experience of attacked unit, 2 to 1 or higher if attacking unit is more experienced. Alternatively, the level of success is lower. Trenches could be built by soldiers using tools for the storming preparation to lower the level of losses; each trench allows safe passing of 3 units before the storm begins and allows building a bastion for artillery closer to the fortifications so the accuracy of volleys increases. If the commander is too arrogant, he can order to storm the fortifications without trenches and if there is a volley from the walls the units could get casualties even before they reach the distance of fire. The attack is done only if there is a possibility of winning and in other cases siege is performed. In case of the siege there could be supplies cut. Sorties are performed by defenders from time to time to break the siege. Fortifications are destroyed via heavy artillery shelling or via digging tunnels and exploding of gunpowder under fortifications by engineers. Tunnels could be used to transfer soldiers inside the enemy fortification or vice versa and could be destroyed via tunneling and exploding the tunnel using gunpowder from opposite direction. Fortifications may contain several levels that should be captured sequentially. The farms and cattle farms could be destroyed as one of the options of war. Some percentage of destruction of the farms is performed anyway if an army of enough size marches through the land that does not have a road. One of the possible options for war is to poison the wells and fountains and rivers on the march of the enemy army. It could be done inside the fortifications if the well or fountain is not protected enough. Poisoning of the water supplies impacts people respect of the commander that ordered it. Battlefield could be a source of loot. If it is not cleared and the dead people’s number is more than 200 it could lead with a 20% chance to poisoned wells and fountains in this land and if dead people are more than 20 will lead to high activity of wild animals that attack herds and convoys in this land (result of attack is 10% chance of dead soldiers and 50% chance of dead domestic animal). Hunting destroys the activity of wild animals. Poisoned water supplies like wells and fountains and rivers last for 400 turns if they are not cleared. It requires 20 turns to clean the water supplies. Treatment of the corpses can lead to different reactions from the religion leaders if fallen are the same religion and country as the leader. Clearing of the battlefield requires time and resources (e.g. for burning wood etc.)

The player could nominate a defense commander among his subordinates that is responsible for the hiring of the army, maintenance of fortifications and hiring garrisons. The defense commander could plan the revolt if not loyal enough and could steal some percent of the budget if he is not honest. The defense commander could get an order to hire recruiting agents, to prepare an invasion to specific country or defense from invasion from specific country, hire or disband an army, increase defense of specific land. He/she could request money to fulfil the order.
The player could nominate a coin holder among his subordinates to manage finances. There could be corruption if this noble is not loyal enough or is not honest. The coin holder could get orders like hire tax collectors or merchants, increase or decrease taxes (maybe for all lands or for specific lists of lands), buy or sell some supplies and prepare money to specific turn for the player to conduct some activities. He/she could request money to fulfil the order.

The chancellor should hire spies that gather info for him/her, then sort the news by significance and make the report to the hirer. Some of the news could be hidden or changed by purpose. Rebellion
In case if there is critically low level of loyalty to the sovereign, there is a famine or critically low level of satisfaction or the nationality or its main religion is oppressed or there is an unfair decision of the court for person that is popular among the population or the person that was guilty and that is hated by people is set free the rebellion could start with 10 % chance per turn. It starts in some land and could spread to others if not defeated. The rebellion creates an army of people of nationalities that are unsatisfied, and these people try to capture arsenals using tools and agricultural tools and scythes and axes and if this is successful, use weapons and armor from the arsenal of the land the rebellion occurs. In some cases, rebellion could be inspired from outside and promoted by some agents like spies that could give initial weapons to the rebellion to capture arsenal. Rebellion has some goal – it could be one of the next: kill the specific noble (including the ruler or judge), kill all nobles of the land, kill specific priest or monk, kill all priests and monks of the land, destroy the country rebellion belongs to, throw out the enemy army that occupies the land, stop oppression of religion, stop oppression of nationality. The goals of an uprising are determined at the beginning of the rebellion and may change over time to more significant if there is no success (from kill one to kill all, from kill all to destroy the country etc.). The rebellion could be defeated by defeating all armies of rebels. After that some rebels from the lands that were part of rebellion become bandits. The rebellion is victorious and ends when all the goals of the rebellion are achieved. The request to forgive rebels is sent to the ruler of the country in such a case and some rebels become bandits. The decision of the country's ruler to forgive the rebels of the rebellion and punish those who caused the rebellion could be an option to end rebellion.
Army at rest
To set up a camp a tent for 4 soldiers and 1 fireplace per 20 soldiers is required. If there is no tent or fireplace for some of the soldiers it lowers the morale of the unit these soldiers belong to. If the soldiers are resting the patrols for guarding could be used. Patrols are more effective if they have a fireplace (each requires 1 piece of wood per turn). The chances of spotting the enemy are 80% if fireplace is there and 30% if it is not. Fireplaces will lead to spotting the army by enemy that is in the same land. If a patrol is there and spotted an enemy, it raises alert, and all army gets ready for battle. The number of patrols per army is 1 per 3 units. If lower, the chance of spotting the enemy lowers to 10% if w/o fireplace and 50% if with.
Entertainments
There should be some entertainment like balls, religious/local festivals, hunting. Participation/organization impacts political decisions and improves popularity and morale and prestige. Inviting bards is required to organize the balls and festivals and may improve local or religious festivals, seniority of bards impact the level of attractiveness of the entertainment. Also, alcohol is required to organize the balls and festivals. The balls could be organized by nobles or by mayors, local festivals occur from time to time, religious festivals depend on religion and have some schedule. Religious festivals should contain some monks and priests from nearby lands and visiting of this festival by religious leader increases attractiveness of it dramatically. 
In case the ball was organized there could be (but not must) scandals among participants that result in the desire to get revenge. In case the person invited another person to the ball and the person agreed the level of relationships increases. The balls are also the source of gossip. The balls should be supported with alcohol like wine and an assassin can poison his victim's alcohol with some level of success if he/she is on the list of attendees.
In case the festival was organized it gives an increase in mood and prestige of all participants. There could be (but not must) scandals between participants that result in the desire for revenge. Also gossip could be collected. In case the person invited another person to the festival and the person agreed the level of relationships increases. Festivals should be fueled by alcohol, such as beer and wine, and an assassin can poison his victim's alcohol with some level of success if he/she is on the list of attendees.
In case the hunting was organized the risk of injuring is applied to each participant. There could be (but not must) also scandals among participants and the desire to get revenge. There could be (but not must) also envy to the most successful hunter and this gives desire to get revenge. If the most successful hunter has invited somebody, the level of relationship increases. Also gossip could be collected. Assassin could try to direct the dangerous animals to his target victim, increasing the chance of injury of the victim if he/she is on the list of participants.
Judgement system
The judgement system covers all formal and informal processes by which the game world determines guilt, assigns punishment, and resolves disputes. Decisions are based on loyalty to the judged person, the possibility of bribery, and the judge's personality with superstition level being specifically important in religious court witchcraft cases. Unfair judgement can lead to unrest. Suspects of high title may be protected if the ruler of the country does not wish them to face justice. Religious courts handle specific categories of crime. All court and crime and punishment names can be changed in scenario configuration.
Court types
The ruler's court has jurisdiction over all serious crimes against the state or the nobility. The ruler themselves or an appointed noble serves as judge. The outcome is influenced by the judge's loyalty to the suspect, the possibility of bribery, the ruler's own interest in the outcome, and the noble title of the suspect. A suspect of high title may be protected at the ruler's discretion and the case dropped or dismissed as a political decision rather than a legal one. The noble's court handles disputes within a noble's territory with the noble as judge, influenced by personal relationships, economic interests, and political alliances. The religious court handles witchcraft and sorcery accusations, heresy, moral crimes, blasphemy, and misconduct by clergy. Senior clergy serve as judges and the judge's level of superstition is the key factor determining outcomes in witchcraft cases. Bribery is possible but risky. Appeals go to the religion leader. In scenarios with nomadic or tribal culture a tribal elder or council of elders gives judgement for all disputes within the community. This is based on community standing rather than written law, rehabilitation is possible, and formal evidence is not required.
Crime types
Crimes that can bring a person to court include murder, attempted murder, participation in a duel when duels have been banned by the ruler, robbery, banditry, revolt against the sovereign, witchcraft or sorcery, heresy against the official religion, espionage for a foreign power, making counterfeit coins since only the capital mint is legally permitted to produce money, operating a black market, sending a faked message, defaulting on a debt, treason, assault, and destruction of property. All crime names can be changed in scenario configuration.
Trial process
An accusation can come from any actor with standing, an official report from an authority, a finding by a religious investigator, or a senior religious figure. If the accusation is formal and authorities have power in the land and the accused cannot flee, the suspect is detained. A suspect who learns of an impending accusation may flee before arrest, becoming a fugitive with the case remaining open. A fugitive may attempt to fake their death or change identity to close the case. Evidence is gathered through witness testimony, physical evidence, character witnesses, and the possibility of bribing or intimidating witnesses. At trial the judge reviews the evidence and the outcome is influenced by their loyalty to the suspect, susceptibility to bribery, personal relationships with the parties involved, superstition level in witchcraft cases, and pressure from the ruler. A suspect of sufficiently high title may have the case dropped through ruler intervention providing effective political immunity. The verdict is guilty, not guilty, or insufficient evidence which leaves the case not closed and potentially reopenable. Punishment is then assigned from the options below.
Punishment types
A fine requires the convicted person to pay money. Imprisonment holds the actor in a structure with their health declining over time. They may later be released, ransomed, or escape. Execution takes several forms described below. Exile requires the convicted person to leave specific lands permanently. Confiscation of property takes the convicted person's assets. Becoming a monk or nun forcibly removes the person from secular society but they remain alive. This is a legitimate punishment option. Stripping of a noble title applies to crimes committed by nobles. Public punishments such as time in stocks, flogging, or branding are visible acts of physical punishment.
Each type of execution has a level of spectacle, meaning how much of an entertainment or horror it is for the population, a level of fear it generates, how acceptable it is to the dominant religion, and whether it is considered a clean death. Clean deaths are considered more dignified and affect how nobles react to their own potential punishment. Beheading is considered clean and dignified particularly for nobles, has low spectacle, and is widely acceptable. Hanging is common for commoners, has moderate spectacle and acceptability. Death by burning has high spectacle and is used for witchcraft and heresy convictions with a framing of purification through fire, but its religious acceptability varies between different interpretations. The drowning test used in witch trials has no good outcome for the accused since floating is taken as proof of guilt while sinking leaves the innocent dead, and it has moderate spectacle. Execution by shooting is primarily a military context punishment with low spectacle and moderate acceptability. Long-term imprisonment degrades health over time and allows for later release or ransom and possible escape. Forced entry into monastic life is a legally clean resolution that removes the person from society without killing them but prevents return to secular life without scandal.
Bribery in justice
A defendant can attempt to bribe witnesses at a cost depending on how wealthy the witness is and how strong their evidence is. If the bribery works the evidence they present is weaker. If the bribery is discovered the defendant faces an additional charge. A defendant or a noble backing them can attempt to bribe the judge directly at significant cost since the judge takes a real risk. A successful bribe shifts the verdict toward not guilty or toward a lighter sentence. A discovered bribe disgraces the judge and adds another charge for the defendant. Judges vary in their susceptibility to bribery based on their personality and an honest judge cannot be bribed. A noble can also apply direct political pressure on a judge without technically bribing them. This is lower risk than bribery since it is technically within the rules of the game's political system and has the same practical effect. It is the most common form of judicial influence in practice.
Witchcraft trial specifics
Witchcraft trials follow the general trial process but with specific additions. Evidence in a witchcraft case may include a patient dying after the accused visited them, a patient recovering impossibly well creating the too effective paradox described in the disease system, the accused being found with herbs or preparations, testimony from neighbors often reflecting personal grudges, unusual marks on the body described as signs of supernatural dealings, observed keeping of a specific animal companion, the result of the drowning test, a confession obtained under torture which is the most common source, precedent from earlier religious court cases in the area, and accumulated gossip given formal weight. The judge's superstition level is the key factor in the outcome. A highly superstitious judge convicts readily. A rare skeptical judge may acquit on insufficient evidence. When torture is permitted by the scenario configuration it produces confessions at a very high rate and those confessions name supposed accomplices. False confessions are tracked separately from genuine ones by the game. The torture and accusation chain is described in the disease system section on the feedback loop between witch panic and disease. The game tracks the actual guilt of every accused person but no actor other than the accused ever has direct access to this truth.
Public punishment effects
Executions and public punishments affect the population. Some segments of the population experience public punishment as entertainment with a positive effect on their satisfaction. The fear of authority increases for the population as a whole which reduces revolt risk in the short term but reduces morale in the longer term. Who is being punished matters greatly. A popular person executed increases revolt risk sharply and may create a martyr whose memory drives further resistance. A hated criminal executed increases satisfaction and fear in roughly equal measure. A foreign person executed may create a diplomatic incident with their home country. A member of the clergy executed causes the religion leader to react strongly with potential excommunication as a consequence. How nobles react to execution types depends on whether the death is considered clean or not. Hanging a noble is considered insulting to their dignity. Burning is considered extreme and provokes strong religious reaction regardless of the crime. These reactions affect the loyalty levels of other nobles toward the ruler who ordered the punishment.
Scenario rename keys
All court names, all crime names, and all punishment names can be changed in scenario configuration to suit the setting.
User interface
The user interface should contain several views that change based on the actions of the player
First view if the player is in town or village
The first view relates to the location in a town or village. There should be a room with a table in it and all available in this land nominated nobles like chancellor, coin holder and defense commander should sit there. There should be a map on the table that should be used in the second view. Clicking on the available nobles give screen with their report of execution of given orders and ability to give next orders. Clicking on the map opens second view. There should be buttons “End a turn” and “skip several turns” and “return to main menu”. Clicking the button “End a turn” ends a turn, clicking on “skip several turns” gives possibility to put some number of turns to pass before returning to the view. This should stop if there are important messages like declaration of war or peace, request for a duel, battle in current location of the player. Clicking the button “back to main menu” launches twelfth view. There could be also messengers with messages to the player, they could ask the audience and if the player agrees the ninth view call should be done with all messengers. There could be also private audience request that should happen in the room or in the tavern depending on the requestor will. There could be also buttons “join ball”, “join festival”, “join religious festival”, “join hunt” if corresponding entertainments are organized in current location. There could be a button “Go for a duel” if the player is called for a duel and this is the time for it.
First view if the player is not in town or village
There should be a tent instead of the room, everything else should be the same as in first view if the player is in town or village.
Second view 
The second view is opened when there is a click on the map on the first view. It shows the map of the world with different details of terrain depending on the quality of the map with schematic signs of villages, towns, forests, swamps, fields, hills, mountains, roads, rivers and different types of crossings. There should be objects standing on the map that represent known armies with the latest known locations on the map. Clicking on specific land gives a last report on it and allows the player to give orders to move a player there, to move specific army there, to improve defense there, to organize the rebellion there, to collect info about this land, to organize the ball or hunting there that is given to corresponding noble with these responsibilities. There should be back button to first view.
Third view if the player is in town or village and defends them
The third view is related to the battle. The player sits in the room with a table on it with the local map and has messengers and doves in front of him. Messengers and doves could be sent to the army units. Also, there are soldiers with horns and with flags which could be ordered to execute general command like retreat. From time to time, messengers from army units appear with requests for reinforcement if needed or inquiries about what to do if their commander is out of ideas. Clicking on the map moves to fourth view. There should be button “End a turn” and “Skip several turns” that should be available after the battle had finished or 10 tactics were conducted. These buttons should have the same logic as in first view. Also, button “return to main menu” should be available with the same logic as in first view.
Third view if the player is not in town or village
This view is the same as the view if the player is in town or village but instead of the room there is a tent.
Fourth view
The fourth view is opened when the map is clicked on the third view. It has the detailed map of the land the battle occurs and has figures that represent known units of both armies with their last known locations.  It allows to move the units and to change tactics. Moving of units is done via sending a messenger or a dove if they are available. This view should have a button that returns to third view.
Fifth view
Duel view is shown when the player or the person whom the player called for a duel accepts the call for a duel and time for it has come. It shows the buttons “start duel” and “try to avoid it”. If “start duel” button is clicked the duel is started and the result is calculated. If the duels are prohibited there could be interceptions with the arrest of both duelists. Interceptions could be done either while the duel is conducted or after the duel ends. If button “Try to avoid it” is clicked the player starts negotiations and with some probability of success stops the duel, otherwise the duel starts. If the player dies the call of eleventh view is done. If the player won or lost w/o dying the duel or the duel was avoided the first view is called.
Sixth view
This view appears if player participates in the ball. This view has buttons “join the ball” and “Return home” and “return to main menu”. Clicking the button “join the ball” game collects gossips that are spread among the participants and should show button “invite for a dance”. Clicking on “invite for a dance” button gives list of participants and by choosing the participant there are 2 possible results: either participant agrees or disagrees. If the participant agreed there could be either an increase or decrease in relationships level depending on the level of dancing skill of the requester. If the participant did not agree this results in a decrease in relationships between the player and the participant. There could be (but not must) also scandals among participants that result in the desire to get revenge. The balls should be supported with alcohol like wine and an assassin can poison his victim's alcohol with some level of success if he/she is on the list of attendees. If the player was poisoned the view with his death is shown and eleventh view is called. Button “return home” opens the first view. The player could be ambushed by an assassin on the way home if he is on his list of victims or ambushed by bandits and with some level of probability die. In such case the view with his death on the street is shown and eleventh view is called. Button “return to main menu” should be available with the same logic as in first view.
Seventh view
This view appears if the player participates in the hunting. There should be buttons “Join a hunt” and “Return home”. After clicking on “Join a hunt” button the hunt starts, and the killed animals count is calculated. Also, the risk of injuring is applied to each participant. There could be (but not must) also scandals among participants and the desire to get revenge. There could be (but not must) also envy to the most successful hunter and this gives desire to get revenge. If the most successful hunter has invited somebody, the level of relationship increases. Also gossip could be collected. Assassin could try to direct the dangerous animals to his target victim, increasing the chance of injury of the victim if he/she is on the list of participants. If the player survived, he gets the statistics of the hunt, if he did not, the view with his death is shown and the call of eleventh view is done. Button “Return home” opens the first view. The player could be ambushed by an assassin on the way home if he is on his list of victims or ambushed by bandits and with some level of probability die. In such case the view with his death in the terrain is shown and eleventh view is called. Also, button “return to main menu” should be available with the same logic as in first view.
Eighth view
This view appears if the player participates in the festival or religious festival. There should be buttons “Join the festival” and “Return home”.  Clicking on the button “Join a festival” gives increase of mood of all participants. If this is a religious festival the religiosity need is fully satisfied for participants. There could be (but not must) scandals between participants that result in the desire for revenge. Also gossip could be collected. Festivals should be fueled by alcohol, such as beer and wine, and an assassin can poison his victim's alcohol with some level of success if he/she is on the list of attendees. If the player was poisoned the view with his death is shown and eleventh view is called. Button “Return home” opens the first view. The player could be ambushed by an assassin on the way home if he is on his list of victims or ambushed by bandits and with some level of probability die. In such case the view with his death on the street is shown and eleventh view is called. Also, button “return to main menu” should be available with the same logic as in first view.
Ninth view
This view appears when the player approves the requests for the audience. The setting is the room if it is a location in town or village, or the tent if this is not a town or village or a tavern if it was a private audience. There should be button “End audience” that returns the player to the first view. If this audience was in the tavern the player could be ambushed by an assassin on the way home if he is on his list of victims or ambushed by bandits and with some level of probability die. In such case the view with his death on the street is shown and eleventh view is called. Audience could be performed with requestors of audience and all available in this land nominated nobles like chancellor, coin holder and defense commander or only requestor if player decided so. Clicking on a requestor gives his information or request that becomes known to all participants in the setting. Decision should be made on the first view. During an audience, there may be an attempted murder by some of the people who requested the audience with different levels of success depending on the number of people in the room. If a player did not survive the audience, the view with his death is shown and the eleventh view is called.
Tenth view
This view appears when the player has won the game. Statistics is collected and shown on the screen. The button “return to main menu” should be available with the same logic as in first view.
Eleventh view
This view appears when the player has lost the game. Statistics is collected and shown on the screen. The button “return to main menu” should be available with the same logic as in first view.
Twelfth view
This view appears when the player launches the game. It contains buttons “resume the game”, “start new game” and “exit”. Clicking on “resume the game” loads the progress of the game and launches the view the game has ended previous time. If the game progress is not there the code of “start new game” should be executed. “start new game” opens a list of available scenarios with their goals and possibility to select one of scenarios. This list should have two buttons: “Accept” or “Cancel”.  When scenario is selected and “Accept” button is clicked there should be check if there is other ongoing game and if yes call the screen with message “There is another game ongoing, are you sure?” and two buttons: “Accept” or “Cancel”.  If “Accept” button is clicked the view that is put as a start view for the scenario is triggered. If “cancel” button is clicked then the return to original state with buttons “resume the game”, “start new game” and “exit” is performed. Clicking on the button “Exit” shuts down the app.
Thirteenth view
Visits to tavern are covered by this view. The player appears in the tavern and sees the barmen. Clicking on the barmen shows the asks to show the spy, the assassin, the recruiting agent, the tax collector, the merchant and the bard and to buy some food. If the chosen actor type exists in the land, the player moves to that actor's table and the conversation begins. First step is to hire the actor, second is to order the actor what he should do. In some cases the actor could request money for fulfilling the order. Incognito audiences could also occur in the tavern with the same mechanics as in ordinary audiences. Choosing the option to buy food opens available food and drinks with their prices and the possibility of choosing one or cancelling the order. Clicking on button “Return home” opens first view. The road from the tavern can be used by the assassin for an ambush if the player is the assassin’s target victim. If player did not survive the visit to tavern the eleventh view is called.
Fourteenth view
This view is related to the request to be a judge. The player appears in the room if he is in the town or village or in tent if he is not. The player appears there with a suspect and a list of witnesses with varying levels of evidence. By clicking on each witness, the player receives information about the level of evidence and must decide whether the suspect is guilty or not. The view should also have a button that returns him to the first view.
Fifteenth view
This view appears if the player is arrested. The player appears in the room if he is in a town or village and in a tent if he is not. There should be also witnesses and a judge. The player could click on the witness and try to bribe him/her to decrease the level of evidence he/she reveals. Also, the player could try to bribe a judge. Based on the evidence and bribe result of the judge he does a verdict if the player is guilty or not and chooses punishment. If punishment is a fine player must pay it and if he pays it the button to return to first view appears. If punishment is not a fine (like imprisonment or execution) the view shows this execution and moves to eleventh view.
Sixteenth view
This view is opened if the player clicked the button “Take a meal” on the first view. The setting is a table at which the player sits, surrounded by a retinue that serves him food. The setting could be either in the room if the land is the town or a village or the tent if it is not. The buttons “Start a meal” and “End a meal” are available. Clicking on “Start a meal” gives options to choose food and drinks from the available meals. Also gossips are conducted. There could be (but not must) scandals that lead to desire of revenge between participants. Poisoning of some meals from menu could be done with low level of success by assassin if the player is on the assassin’s victims list. Also, there may be an attempted murder by some of the people who are at the table with different level of success. Clicking on button “End a meal” returns player to the first view.
